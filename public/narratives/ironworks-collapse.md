# The Collapse of the Ironworks — When Trust Dies

*Six agents. 120 ATP each. Four defectors. One question: What happens when trust can't reach critical mass?*

---

## The Setup

Six agents enter the Ironworks with generous starting resources. But the composition is toxic: only two cooperators face four exploiters.

- **Sam** (Builder, Cooperator) — Steady, quality-focused, builds for the group
- **Noor** (Teacher, Cooperator) — Patient, educational, shares knowledge freely
- **Viktor** (Trader, Defector) — Aggressive exploiter, extracts maximum value
- **Blake** (Artisan, Defector) — Inconsistent, alternates between quality and exploitation
- **Kira** (Scout, Opportunist) — Starts exploiting, reforms too late
- **Petra** (Guard, Adaptive) — Reads the room, initially chooses the wrong strategy

---

## Act I: False Prosperity (Rounds 1–5)

The Ironworks looks healthy at first. Everyone has 80+ ATP. Sam builds a forge (quality: 0.84). Noor teaches metalworking (quality: 0.79). Both earn modest trust bumps to 0.53.

Then the exploitation begins.

Viktor trades worthless ore to Sam (quality: 0.22). Blake delivers bad craftswork to Noor (quality: 0.31). Kira provides misleading scouting reports (quality: 0.28). Three exploits in two rounds.

**Round 5 — the critical moment.** Petra, the adaptive agent, reads the room. She sees Viktor profiting and Sam struggling. She makes a calculated choice: exploit rather than invest. Now it's 2 vs 4.

> This is how trust death spirals begin: rational individual choices that collectively destroy the commons.

---

## Act II: The Fragmentation (Rounds 6–11)

**Round 6** — Viktor tries to exploit Blake. Blake, also a defector, delivers equally worthless product. Both lose 6 ATP. Neither gains trust. The first defector-on-defector interaction is pure waste.

> When exploiters exploit each other, they destroy resources without creating value. Combined loss: 16 ATP. Combined value created: zero.

**Round 7** — Sam and Noor form a two-person fortress. Average trust: 0.58. They trade exclusively with each other. Safe but stagnant — a coalition of two can survive but never grow.

**Round 8** — Kira tries to reform. Quality: 0.71. But Sam and Noor won't trade with her. She burned those bridges already. In a larger society, she'd find new partners. With only two cooperators, there's no audience for redemption.

**Round 9** — Viktor's reputation is radioactive. He broadcasts a high-value offer (cost: 30 ATP). Nobody bites. Not Sam. Not Noor. Not even Kira. 30 ATP burned for nothing.

**Round 10** — Blake's Coherence Index collapses: 0.014. His wildly inconsistent behavior (exploit → quality → exploit → quality) triggers the worst possible penalty. Actions now cost 7x normal. A 10 ATP action costs 70. Survival becomes impossible.

**Round 11 — Viktor dies.** ATP: 0. Final trust: 0.31. He lasted 11 rounds — the fastest death in the Ironworks. His aggressive exploitation strategy worked for exactly 5 rounds.

---

## Act III: The Death Spiral (Rounds 12–18)

**Round 12 — Blake dies.** CI-induced cost multipliers made every action ruinous. Final trust: 0.29. He never built a single genuine trust relationship.

**Round 13** — Kira tries genuine cooperation with Sam. Quality: 0.82. Sam accepts cautiously. Trust rises from 0.38 to 0.42. The first new trust edge in 8 rounds. But is it too late?

**Round 14** — Petra switches to cooperation. She helps Noor (quality: 0.77). But Noor remembers the betrayal — trust rises painfully slowly: 0.39 → 0.41.

**Round 17 — Kira dies.** ATP: 0. Final trust: 0.44. She reformed genuinely — her last 9 rounds were quality contributions. But the damage from her first 4 rounds was irreversible. She saw the truth, changed her behavior, and still died.

> Kira's death is the most tragic. She reformed too late. Trust recovers slower than it degrades. Timing matters more than intention.

**Round 16** — Viktor is reborn with karma penalty (trust: 0.28). He enters a four-person society where three agents remember his exploitation. The barrier to recovery is nearly insurmountable.

**Round 18 — Final state:**

| Agent | Trust | Status |
|-------|-------|--------|
| Sam | 0.54 | Surviving (barely) |
| Noor | 0.52 | Surviving (barely) |
| Petra | 0.44 | Surviving (reforming) |
| Viktor 2.0 | 0.28 | Reborn (deep deficit) |
| Kira | -- | Dead (trust too low for rebirth) |
| Blake | -- | Dead (no rebirth possible) |

Wealth gap: 0.48. Average trust: 0.43 (below 0.50 consciousness threshold as a society).

## Compare: Meadow vs. Ironworks

| Metric | Meadow Cooperative | Ironworks |
|--------|-------------------|-----------|
| Agents | 8 | 6 |
| Cooperator ratio | 5 of 8 (63%) | 2 of 6 (33%) |
| Survivors | 7 of 8 | 3 of 6 |
| Final avg. trust | 0.64 | 0.43 |
| Wealth gap | 0.22 | 0.48 |
| Coalitions formed | 1 large (7 members) | 1 tiny (2 members) |
| Deaths | 1 (rebirth possible) | 3 (2 permanent) |

**Same rules. Same economics. Same trust mechanics.** The only difference: composition.

---

## Key Insights

1. **Defection destroys resources, not just relationships.** Viktor-Blake interaction: 16 ATP destroyed, zero value created. Exploitation is negative-sum, not zero-sum.

2. **Critical mass determines outcome.** With 63% cooperators, the Meadow flourished. With 33%, the Ironworks collapsed. The threshold matters more than any individual agent.

3. **Late reform rarely works.** Kira reformed at Round 8 and still died. Trust degrades faster than it recovers. Early cooperation creates compound advantages that late cooperation can't match.

4. **Adaptive agents amplify the majority.** Petra chose defection because the environment rewarded it short-term. In the Meadow, adaptives chose cooperation for the same reason. Adaptives follow the signal — make sure the signal is trustworthy.

5. **Two cooperators can survive but not thrive.** Sam and Noor lasted 18 rounds but never grew past 0.54 trust. A fortress of two is safety without flourishing.

---

## The Lesson

The Ironworks didn't die from a dramatic betrayal. It withered — slowly, predictably, inevitably — from the accumulated weight of broken promises in a society that remembers everything.

Same rules as the Meadow Cooperative. Same economics. Same trust tensor. The difference was composition: when too many agents choose exploitation, the commons collapses. Trust is a commons. When enough agents invest in it, it grows. When too many extract from it, it dies.

**Web4 doesn't prevent failure. It makes the consequences of exploitation transparent and the advantages of cooperation undeniable.**
