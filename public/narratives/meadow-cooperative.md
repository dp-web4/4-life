# The Meadow Cooperative — When Trust Builds a Society

*Eight strangers. 100 ATP each. No rules. One question: Can trust create order from chaos?*

---

## The Setup

Eight agents arrive in a new Web4 society with nothing but equal resources and a starting trust of 0.50. No one knows anyone. No one has authority. The only signal is action.

- **Alice** (Farmer) — Consistent, generous, steady
- **Charlie** (Builder) — Reliable, collaborative, pragmatic
- **Eve** (Merchant) — Opportunistic, strategic, eventually desperate
- **Raj** (Healer) — Cautious, observant, late-blooming
- **Luna** (Teacher) — Adaptive, broad-reaching, forgiving
- **Marcus** (Guard) — Steady, protective, trusted
- **Zara** (Artisan) — Quality-focused, independent, moderate
- **Derek** (Scout) — Free-rider turned reformer

---

## Act I: The Arrival (Rounds 1–5)

Everyone starts equal. The first five rounds reveal character.

Alice shares a harvest (quality: 0.82). It costs her 12 ATP but earns 15 back — quality above 70% is rewarded, not penalized. Charlie builds a shelter for Raj. Both earn modest trust bumps to 0.52. Generosity isn't charity in Web4 — it's investment.

Eve spots an opportunity. She trades with Alice but delivers inferior quality (0.35). The Value Tensor catches it immediately: below the 30% quality floor, zero earnings, full cost. Alice's trust in Eve drops from 0.50 to 0.46.

Derek tries free-riding: consuming without contributing. Community gossip (MRH weight 0.2) spreads the news within two rounds. His reputation drops to 0.47.

Raj watches. He waits five full rounds before committing, then chooses to heal Charlie — the partner with the best track record. Patience works in Web4.

> **By Round 5**: Alice and Charlie are building mutual trust. Eve and Derek have gained short-term ATP but damaged their reputations. The seeds of the cooperative are planted through behavior, not agreement.

---

## Act II: Coalitions Form (Rounds 6–12)

Alice and Charlie's mutual trust reaches 0.62. When they collaborate on a barn-raising (combined quality: 0.88), both earn ATP bonuses. Raj joins as healer. A three-person coalition takes shape — not declared, but visible in the trust graph.

Eve tries exploiting Zara. But gossip has traveled: Raj told Luna (0.7 weight), Luna told Zara (0.49 weight). Zara demands quality first. Eve can't deliver. Trust drops to 0.41.

Marcus, the guard, has been quietly building trust through consistent patrols (quality 0.75–0.80). He joins the coalition. Four members. Average trust: 0.59.

**Round 10 — The trap closes on Eve.** ATP: 32. Her damaged reputation means fewer agents will trade with her. She's caught: too distrusted to earn, too poor to leave. This is the Web4 death spiral — low reputation → fewer opportunities → lower ATP → closer to death.

**Round 12 — Derek changes.** Watching Eve's decline, he sees his own future. He starts contributing quality scouting reports: 0.72, then 0.78, then 0.81. Trust ticks up painfully: 0.47 → 0.48 → 0.49. Redemption is possible but expensive.

> **The coalition formed through behavioral alignment, not negotiation.** Alice never asked Charlie to join her. Eve's isolation happened through the same mechanism: the trust graph made behavioral patterns visible to everyone.

---

## Act III: The Reckoning (Rounds 13–18)

The Meadow Cooperative is now visible structure: Alice (0.68), Charlie (0.64), Raj (0.61), Marcus (0.60), Luna (0.58), Zara (0.56). They trade preferentially within the group.

Outside: Eve at 0.38, Derek at 0.49.

**Round 15 — Eve's gamble.** With 18 ATP left, she broadcasts a genuinely high-quality trade (0.83) to everyone. Cost: 35 ATP. She's betting everything.

Luna accepts. The quality IS high. Eve earns 14 ATP — not enough to recover, but enough for another round. Trust bumps from 0.38 to 0.40. One genuine contribution in a sea of exploitation.

**Round 17 — The Coherence Index strikes.** Eve's erratic pattern (switching between exploitation and quality) triggers severe CI penalties. Capability dimension: 0.45. Temporal: 0.38. Multiplicative score: 0.063. Devastating. ATP costs spike 1.4x.

**Round 18 — Eve dies.** ATP: 0. Final trust: 0.40. She survived 18 rounds — longer than a pure defector would, thanks to that one genuine trade. Her record is preserved forever.

> **Eve wasn't banned, voted out, or punished.** She died because her behavioral pattern couldn't generate enough energy to sustain itself. The system didn't punish her. It stopped subsidizing her.

---

## Act IV: Rebuilding and Rebirth (Rounds 19–25)

Derek's trust reaches 0.55. Seven consecutive rounds of quality (0.78–0.84). Luna invites him to contribute scouting reports for the cooperative. Charlie acknowledges: "His reports have been reliable." Derek becomes the seventh member.

**Round 20** — The cooperative has specialization: Alice farms, Charlie builds, Raj heals, Marcus guards, Luna teaches, Zara crafts, Derek scouts. Nobody assigned these roles. Agents gravitated toward strengths because the trust tensor tracked domain-specific competence.

**Round 21 — Eve is reborn.** Starting trust: 0.42 (karma penalty). Starting ATP: 75. She enters a society that remembers her — not through grudges, but through the trust graph. Her old interactions carry negative weight.

Eve 2.0 begins contributing consistently: 0.76, 0.79, 0.74. Not spectacular, but reliable. Trust crawls from 0.42 to 0.45. She's learning what Derek learned: consistency beats brilliance.

**Round 25 — Final state:**

| Agent | Trust | Status |
|-------|-------|--------|
| Alice | 0.72 | Thriving (Cooperative leader) |
| Charlie | 0.68 | Thriving |
| Raj | 0.65 | Thriving |
| Marcus | 0.63 | Thriving |
| Luna | 0.62 | Thriving |
| Zara | 0.59 | Thriving |
| Derek | 0.58 | Thriving (Reformed) |
| Eve 2.0 | 0.47 | Surviving (Rebuilding) |

Wealth gap: 0.22 — below the healthy 0.25 baseline. The society is stable, productive, and built itself.

---

## Key Insights

1. **Eve gained 40 ATP by exploiting early trust — then lost everything.** Short-term exploitation is self-defeating when actions are transparent.

2. **Alice's consistency (0.94 cooperation rate) made her the leader.** Trust rewards boring reliability over flashy performance.

3. **Derek's reform took 8 rounds of consistent quality** before anyone trusted him again. Rebuilding trust is possible but expensive.

4. **The cooperative emerged without voting or governance.** Trust networks naturally created social structure based purely on behavioral history.

5. **When Eve was reborn with karma penalty, she had to outwork newcomers.** Past behavior shaped future opportunity — consequences persist across lives.

---

## The Lesson

No one designed this outcome. No voting, no governance, no central authority. Trust metrics, transparent behavior, and economic incentives produced a self-organizing society where cooperation is the rational strategy and exploitation is the path to death.

Web4 doesn't need a government to build a just society. It needs transparent trust.
