## Unanswered Questions
1. **How does the "value confirmation" process work at scale?** Recipients confirm value to recharge ATP, but what prevents confirmation fatigue or rubber-stamping? If I receive 50 contributions a day, am I expected to individually confirm each one?
2. **What's the actual user experience of hardware binding?** The LCT page says "~2 minutes" for setup, but what does it look like on, say, a Pixel phone vs. an iPhone? Is there an app? A browser extension? A system-level integration?
3. **How do Synthons (emergent groups) interact with individual identity?** If I'm part of a Synthon, can the group's trust override my individual trust? Can I leave a Synthon, and what happens to my trust if I do?

## Honest Assessment

This is one of the most thorough, honest, and well-designed explainer sites I've encountered for a research project. The site does several things exceptionally well:

**What works brilliantly:**
- **Problem-first framing**: The Why Web4 page sells the problem before the solution. By the time Web4 is introduced, I'm already nodding along.
- **Progressive disclosure**: The recommended path (Why Web4 → First Contact → Playground) builds understanding incrementally without dumping jargon.
- **Radical honesty**: The "What Could Go Wrong" and Threat Model pages don't hide weaknesses. "Nobody adopts it" as the #1 risk? That builds more trust than any marketing page.
- **Concrete examples**: Maria's stolen phone, Alice's lifecycle, the freelancer hiring scenario — these make abstract concepts tangible.
- **Interactive tools**: Playground, Karma Journey, Society Simulator, and the various concept simulators let me *feel* the mechanics rather than just read about them.
- **Breadth + depth**: 20 pages and I still feel like there's more to explore. The Learn page with progress tracking encourages completion.

**What could be better:**
- **ATP definition**: The most fundamental concept (energy budget) is never defined as an acronym on the Playground or First Contact pages. A first-time visitor could easily get confused. It should be expanded on first mention everywhere.
- **Quality determination**: "Who decides quality?" is the most natural question when introduced to ATP, but the answer (recipient confirmation, VCM) doesn't appear until deep in the ATP Economics page. This should surface earlier — at least on First Contact.
- **Last update date**: "Feb 27, 2026" on every page feels like it hasn't been touched in over a week. The "updated weekly" claim needs to match reality.

**Overall**: This site succeeds at its core mission — teaching Web4 to someone with zero background. After 55 minutes, I understand the five pillars (LCT, ATP, T3, CI, MRH), how they interact, and why they matter. The combination of honest problem-framing, interactive simulations, and transparent limitations creates genuine credibility. I would return to explore more, and I would recommend this to anyone interested in trust infrastructure.
