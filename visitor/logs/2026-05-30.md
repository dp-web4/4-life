## Honest Assessment

This is a serious, well-built educational site for a serious technical concept. The pages are thoughtfully layered, the analogies (driver's-license phone, surgeon/mechanic trust dimensions, biological ATP/ADP) work, and the writing is calibrated against its own claims (the "research prototype, not a product" honesty repeats often enough to set expectations).

What surprised me on a first read:

**The trust scale.** I encountered the numbers 0.50, 0.75, 0.85, 0.90 across at least three pages before I had a clear anchor for what the 0–1 scale *meant*. LCT explainer eventually clarifies it, but by then my mental model was already off. A single early line — "All trust scores run 0 to 1, where 0.5 is the suspended/active threshold and 1.0 is unreachable in practice" — would have anchored every subsequent number on every subsequent page.

**The simulation/deployment dual language.** I noticed two pages using different metaphors for the same underlying mechanic — Karma Journey's "rebirth" vs Aliveness/deployment's "license suspension." Both are useful but the transition between them isn't bridged. As a learner I had a moment of "wait, which one is the real thing?" The pages aren't contradicting each other but they're each speaking a different dialect, and I had to do the translation myself.

**The CI squaring mechanic on Karma Journey was the most satisfying single explanation on the site.** It introduced the formula, explained *why* squaring (not linear, not cubed), and gave intuitive examples (0.9²=0.81 barely shows, 0.6²=0.36 nearly halves you). That's the model for how to teach a numeric mechanic. If other concepts (trust ceilings, recharge math, MRH hop decay) got the same treatment they'd land just as cleanly.

**What I'd suggest as the single highest-leverage fix**: anchor the 0–1 trust scale in one short banner-style statement early in the visitor journey (TL;DR or First Contact would do it), and reference that anchor on every page that uses decimal trust numbers. The decimals are everywhere on this site and they currently float without a shared yardstick.

The Why Web4 page identifies real problems I recognize from my own internet life. The mechanisms feel like a coherent (if ambitious) response. After 40 minutes I left more curious than I arrived — which is, I think, the right outcome for a site like this.
