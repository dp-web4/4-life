## Journey

### Landing Page (00:00)
- First impression: Clear and confident. "4-Life: A Trust-Native Internet Lab — explore Web4 through simulations." The one-line definition of Web4 ("trust verified by behavior, not assumed by default; no blockchain, not a Web3 successor") is genuinely helpful — it pre-empts my two biggest wrong guesses (crypto / Web3) in the first breath. Good.
- The framing headline "The Internet Has a Trust Problem" landed immediately. Spam, siloed reputation, free resets, AI deception — I recognized all of these.
- Confusion points: A cluster of acronyms (LCT, ATP, T3, MRH, CI) appears with only a promise of "full definitions in the glossary." I didn't understand any of them yet, but the page told me where they'd be explained, so I wasn't lost — just holding my breath.
- Clicked: "Start here: 2-minute TL;DR →" (exactly what I was invited to do).

### 2-Minute TL;DR (03:00)
- The promise up front — "Plain English first... every term is spelled out the first time it appears" — set a good expectation, and it mostly delivered. ATP got "an energy budget for online actions," LCT got "your device-anchored identity."
- The three pillars (actions cost energy; identity anchors to hardware; reputation follows you) were the first thing that actually *clicked* for me.
- Confusion: "constellation" (devices vouching for each other) and "Markov Relevancy Horizon" both felt jargony even in a 2-minute summary. I didn't understand *why* hardware identity matters yet — that came later.
- Clicked: "Why Web4?" to understand the problem in full before the solution.

### Why Web4? (06:00)
- The problem framing is the best writing on the site. "You already know this. You feel it every day" — yes. The five failures are concrete, and the section explaining why past fixes (moderation, CAPTCHAs, blockchain wallets, social login) failed built real credibility.
- **BUG — garbled text.** Right in the intro I hit: **"Running Running nowthis isn't only theory — hestia (the trust layer) and the hub (a runnable Web4 society) are public, AGPL code..."** The word "Running" is duplicated and "now" is jammed into "this" ("nowthis"). This is broken text on one of the most important pages, and it made me trust the site slightly *less* right at the moment it was trying to prove Web4 is real, not vaporware. (I later found the SAME broken pattern on the Trust Tensor page — see below — so this is a shared component, not a one-off typo.)
- Acronym clustering: a card section fires ATP, LCT, T3, MRH at me in quick succession, each with a one-line "just means..." gloss, and then a recap sentence uses all four plus CI at once. The page even says "Lost in the acronyms? Quick index" — which honestly reads like the page knows it overloaded me.
- The "Honest Questions" FAQ is excellent but *enormous* — longer than the page itself. New terms (Sybil attacks, Federation, V3/Value Tensor, zero-knowledge proofs) first appear down there without prior intro.
- Clicked: "First Contact" (the walkthrough), hoping a narrative would tie the acronyms together.

### First Contact (11:00)
- This is an interactive, driveable simulation (you steer "Alice" across a 5-act, ~7-minute arc). The premise is appealing and the pacing controls (pause/scrub/replay, nothing saved, no account) lowered my anxiety.
- The page promises concepts "are explained the moment Alice uses it" — but *before* the walkthrough there's a reference map that front-loads ATP, T3, LCT, CI with terse shorthand. It says "you don't need to memorize these," which helped, but I still met the acronyms before their story context.
- **MRH** shows up in the top nav ("Core Concepts") but never appears on this page — so I'm still holding an undefined acronym from the landing page.
- I didn't run the full 5-act sim in this browse, but the setup was inviting enough that I'd come back for it.

### Karma Journey (16:00)
- Interactive and immediately clear what to do: 8 action buttons ("Help a peer," "Take a shortcut," "Rest"...), each with an energy cost, and a live stat panel (Raw Trust 0.500, Effective Trust 0.361, Energy 100, Consistency 0.85).
- Nice touch: Raw vs Effective trust are explicitly contrasted (raw "decides survival," effective "decides karma tier"), and the 1.4× new-identity energy surcharge is explained ("no history yet, so it costs more"). That surcharge explanation was a small "oh, clever" moment.
- Friction: the formula **"Effective trust = raw × CI²"** shows up without inline intuition for *why it's squared* right where it first appears (it's explained lower down, but not at first sight). As a newcomer, "squared" felt arbitrary in the moment.
- Karma tiers (Honored > 0.7, Neutral 0.3–0.7, Constrained < 0.3) appear without a lead-in.
- No garbled text here.

### A Day in Web4 (20:00)
- My favorite page for *concreteness*. Ten everyday scenarios; the first ("Your Phone Wakes Up") gives real branching choices, and each scenario links its jargon to an explainer. This is the "so what does this feel like to actually use" answer I'd been wanting.
- ATP is defined right at first mention ("your energy budget for taking actions — every post or reply spends some, and useful contributions earn it back"). Good.
- Friction: "distance-adjusted trust fades with distance" uses jargon without an inline definition, and trust ceilings (0.85) are dropped without context. After reading, the "what do I do next" path felt a little unclear — it offers links back to Why Web4 / First Contact / simulators but no single obvious "next."

### Trust Tensor (T3) (23:00)
- The core idea landed cleanly thanks to a great line: "one number couldn't tell a brilliant-but-erratic surgeon from a kind doctor with shaky hands. Three numbers can." The role-context point (Alice-as-analyst ≠ Alice-as-mechanic) was hammered home well.
- **BUG — same garbled text as Why Web4.** I hit **"Running Running nowhestia evolves real T3/V3..."** — identical corruption ("Running Running now" + text jammed on with no space). Seeing the exact same break on a second page confirmed for me it's a broken shared component, and it's presumably on every page that carries that "Running now" callout.
- Minor: the update formula `base = 0.02 × (quality − 0.5)` reappears despite the page saying "you don't need the math." I didn't need it and it briefly made me feel I was missing something.

### ATP Economics (25:00)
- Solidly taught. ATP = "your energy budget," ADP = "the receipt / proof your work happened, you never hold or manage it." The biological-ATP parallel is genuinely intuitive. Quality ramp (weak work earns little, strong work earns ~7×) was memorable.
- Friction: the 5% transfer fee ("sender pays 105, Bob receives 100") is stated twice but I still re-read it. And "ATP sounds like a currency but isn't tradeable" is a distinction the metaphor works against — I had to consciously correct myself.

## Unanswered Questions
1. How exactly does hardware-bound identity (LCT) stop someone from just... making a new device identity? What's the actual barrier to a reset? (The whole model hinges on this and I never got a crisp answer in my reading path.)
2. If reputation follows you everywhere permanently, what stops one bad day / one false accusation from ruining you forever? (I saw "warning dip vs permanent collapse" mentioned but didn't fully grasp the safety margin.)
3. What actually *is* the "constellation" of devices vouching for each other, and do I need more than one device to participate?
4. Is anyone actually using this? The "Running now" callout tries to answer (public AGPL code, no live network yet) — but it's the broken-text callout, so the answer arrived garbled.

## Honest Assessment
The site is genuinely good and clearly cared-for. The problem framing on "Why Web4?" is the strongest thing here — it made me *want* the solution before showing it. ATP and Trust Tensor both taught me their core ideas with analogies that stuck (energy budget; surgeon-vs-doctor). "A Day in Web4" was the page that made it feel real rather than academic.

Two things held me back from "solid" understanding. First, **acronym density** — after the landing page I was carrying five undefined initialisms, and the recap sentences that fire all of them at once made me feel behind rather than caught up. This didn't stop me, but it's a low-grade tax on every page.

Second, and more concretely: the **"Running Running now" garbled text**. I hit it on Why Web4 and again, identically, on Trust Tensor. It's a small bug in isolation, but it lands on the exact sentence trying to convince me Web4 is *real code, not theory* — so a rendering glitch there does outsized damage to credibility. It's also clearly a shared component, so it's probably quietly broken across many pages. That's the one thing I'd fix first.

I'd come back — mostly to actually run the First Contact walkthrough and the Karma Journey, which I only set up but didn't play through. I'd recommend it, but with a caveat: send a newcomer to the TL;DR or "A Day in Web4" first, not straight to "Why Web4?", or the acronym wall may lose them.
