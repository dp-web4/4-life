## Honest Assessment
This is a genuinely well-made educational site. The progressive entry ramps (TL;DR → First Contact → Why Web4) are excellent, the analogies are concrete (credit check, bankruptcy vs. losing a license, cash vs. receipt), and the honesty about being research-not-product builds trust rather than eroding it. I got to "good" comprehension in 40 minutes across 10 pages without prior knowledge — that's strong.

The friction that stuck out is a single cross-cutting pattern, not a page-specific bug: **two concepts (CI-squared and V3) are perpetually deferred.** Every page that touches them shows the symbol or names the term, then says "explained elsewhere." Individually each deferral is reasonable; collectively, a naive visitor hits the same two unexplained things on four different pages and starts to feel they're being kept just out of reach. The highest-leverage fix isn't a new page — it's a tiny inline gloss at each point of first contact so the deferral never compounds. The V3 deferral on trust-tensor is the sharper of the two, because the page itself calls V3 "the lever your reputation actually moves on" in the same paragraph it tells me to ignore it — that internal contradiction is what made it memorable.
