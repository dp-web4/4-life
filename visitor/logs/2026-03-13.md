2. **What does the actual UI look like?** — The LCT page describes "Open app on phone" but there's no mockup or wireframe showing what a Web4 app would actually look like in practice. The "Day in Web4" scenarios describe the experience but I never see it.
3. **How does cross-society trust transfer actually work?** — Federation is mentioned but the mechanics of "your reputation travels with you" across different communities are vague. If I have 0.91 Talent as a data analyst in Community A, how does Community B know about that?
4. **What prevents a society from setting unfair rules?** — The SAL governance framework is mentioned but I'm unclear on checks and balances. What if a society's "law oracle" is biased?

## Honest Assessment

This is one of the most thoughtfully constructed educational sites I've encountered on a technical topic. The progression from "here's the problem" to "here's the idea" to "try it yourself" is well-executed, and the site takes the visitor's confusion seriously.

**What worked best:**
- The TL;DR page is exactly what I needed first. It should be even more prominent.
- "A Day in Web4" scenarios made the abstract concrete. I finally *felt* why this matters when reading about spam costing energy and fake reviews being detectable.
- "What Could Go Wrong" is the single most trust-building page. Publishing your own failure analysis is rare and impressive. If I were skeptical, this is the page that would keep me reading.
- The four featured narratives (Meadow, Ironworks, Garden, Tidepool) demonstrate outcomes from composition beautifully.
- Interactive elements (Playground, Karma Journey, Simulator) provide genuine experimentation.

**What could improve:**
- MRH is a significant gap in the concept sequence. It's one of Web4's 4 core mechanisms but gets no dedicated explainer. I understand LCT, ATP, T3, CI, and Aliveness but context boundaries remain fuzzy.
- The last update date (Mar 6) is stale for a site claiming weekly updates. Small thing but it signals "abandoned project" to a skeptic.
- Trust ceiling numbers need reconciliation between the LCT page sections.

**Overall:** The site successfully taught me a complex system from zero knowledge. I arrived confused, and left understanding the core loop (hardware identity → energy budget → trust dimensions → coherence → aliveness). The problem framing is compelling, the solutions are clearly explained, and the honesty about what's unsolved builds genuine credibility. I would return to play more with the simulations.
