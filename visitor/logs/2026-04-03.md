## Unanswered Questions

2. **How does trust transfer work in practice during a platform migration?** The site says "your trust moves with you" but the technical details of how a platform would import/verify a trust history from a federated network aren't clear. What does the API call look like?
3. **What happens to trust scores during prolonged offline periods?** I know trust decays with half-lives (Talent: 365d, Training: 180d, Temperament: 30d), but what about someone who goes offline for 6 months — do they return to find their Temperament trust essentially zeroed? Is there a "hiatus" mechanism?
4. **How does the 5% ATP transfer tax interact with team collaboration?** If a team of 5 people frequently transfers ATP for collaborative projects, the compound 5% burn seems like it could become significant. Is there a team/organizational structure that avoids this overhead?

## Honest Assessment

This is one of the most thorough and well-structured educational sites I've encountered for a technical research project. What stands out most:

**What works exceptionally well:**
- **Problem-first framing everywhere.** Every page starts with "why" before "what" or "how." The landing page, Why Web4, ATP Economics — they all lead with pain points I recognize.
- **Progressive disclosure.** The 2-minute TL;DR → Why Web4 → First Contact → Deep concept pages is a natural escalation. I never felt thrown into the deep end.
- **Honest caveats.** The site consistently says "this is research, not a product." The What Could Go Wrong page is genuinely self-critical. This builds credibility.
- **Concrete examples and interactive tools.** Alice's journey, the daily scenarios, the app mockups, the Karma Journey choices — these make abstract concepts tangible.
- **The Narratives page.** The four society stories (Meadow, Ironworks, Garden, Tidepool) are the most memorable content on the site. The Tidepool insight about reciprocators is genuinely surprising.
- **Consistent terminology.** I never felt confused about what a term meant. The glossary panel is always accessible, and terms are defined inline when first introduced.

**What could improve:**
- The "What's New" section on the landing page showing only March dates might signal staleness to an April visitor. A minor but real first-impression issue.
- The What Could Go Wrong page could rank its risks — some feel existential (adoption failure, scalability) while others feel more manageable (device loss, hardware lock-in). Helping visitors understand which risks the team worries about most would add transparency.
- The EP/Epistemic Proprioception terminology on the Compare page is a jarring moment of jargon in an otherwise accessible page. "Cross-Life Learning" is the better label and should be used consistently.

**Overall:** The site successfully took me from "what is this?" to "I understand the core mechanics and why they matter" in about 45 minutes. The combination of narrative walkthroughs, interactive tools, concrete examples, and honest caveats makes this genuinely educational rather than just informational. I'd return to explore the Society Simulator more deeply and try the Playground experiments.
