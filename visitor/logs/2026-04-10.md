2. Is ATP a currency (you buy things with it) or just an attention budget (it limits your actions)? The Day in Web4 marketplace pricing implies currency, but the rest of the site treats it as an energy budget.

## Honest Assessment

This is a genuinely impressive site for an active research project. The content is deep, the interactive elements are thoughtful, and the writing quality is high. The "Day in Web4" page alone is worth the visit — it takes abstract protocol mechanics and makes them feel like a real product you'd want to use.

**What works well:**
- The progressive entry points (2-min, 5-min, etc.) respect my time
- Alice's journey in First Contact is the best single teaching tool
- The site is refreshingly honest about what exists vs. what doesn't
- Biological metaphors (energy, death, rebirth) make technical concepts intuitive
- The app mockups in Day in Web4 are compelling and concrete

**What needs work:**
- **The MRH page is broken.** This is a core concept that I literally cannot learn about. That's a showstopper for the learning path.
- **Acronym overload.** By page 3, I had ATP, ADP, LCT, T3, V3, MRH, CI, SAL, and DID. The site uses progressive disclosure for concepts but dumps acronyms all at once. Consider using plain English longer (e.g., "energy budget" instead of "ATP") before introducing the acronym.
- **Page length varies wildly.** The TL;DR page is perfectly scoped. The Aliveness page is a 20-minute read that covers individual agents, energy states, birth ceremonies, key rotation, appeals mechanisms, AND emergent group consciousness. That's too much for one page.
- **The effective trust vs. raw trust distinction needs earlier, clearer treatment.** It creates genuine confusion in the Karma Journey when your effective trust starts below the death threshold.

**Overall:** I came in knowing nothing about Web4. After 25 minutes, I understand the core idea (trust built into internet infrastructure via energy costs, hardware identity, multi-dimensional reputation, and consequence persistence) and I find it genuinely interesting. The simulation/interactive elements make me want to explore more. I'd return to try the Society Simulator and Karma Journey properly. Whether I'd recommend it depends on the audience — a technically curious friend, yes; my parents, not yet.
