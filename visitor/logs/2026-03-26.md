## Unanswered Questions
1. **What does the actual user experience look like for key management?** The Day in Web4 mockups show the ideal end state, but what does the onboarding process look like? When I first set up Web4, what do I actually *do* with my phone's security chip? Is there an app? A browser extension? This is still abstract.
2. **How does cross-society trust transfer work in practice?** The MRH page mentions federation at "~65% weight" discount, but what determines that discount? Who decides which societies to federate with? Is it automatic or manual?
3. **What happens to trust during a platform migration?** If a platform goes from Tier 0 (no Web4) to Tier 1 (wrapper), what happens to existing users' years of platform-specific reputation? Is it imported into T3, or do they start fresh?
4. **How are V3 scores actually computed?** The trust tensor page explains V3 dimensions (Valuation, Veracity, Validity) but doesn't show *who* scores Veracity and Validity. The VCM handles Valuation (recipient confirms), but who checks if something is *truthful* or *well-reasoned*?

## Honest Assessment

This is an exceptionally well-crafted educational site about a genuinely ambitious research project. The progressive disclosure works — I went from "what is this?" to "I understand the full system" in under an hour without feeling lost at any point.

**What works best:**
- **Problem-first framing**: Every page starts with why before what. The landing page's "The Internet Has a Trust Problem" immediately resonated.
- **Day in Web4 app mockups**: The single most effective content on the site. Seeing Web4 Mail with trust scores next to each sender communicated more than any amount of theory.
- **Honest self-assessment**: The "What Could Go Wrong" page is a masterclass in building credibility. Admitting "Nobody adopts it" is the #1 risk made me trust the project more, not less.
- **The four featured narratives**: The Tidepool finding (29% cooperators + reciprocators outperforming 100% cooperators) was genuinely surprising and made me think differently about cooperation dynamics.
- **Concept sequence**: LCT → ATP → T3 → MRH → CI → Aliveness builds logically. Each concept makes the next one easier to understand.

**What could be better:**
- **The onboarding UX question persists**: I now understand the *theory* thoroughly, but I still don't know what it would feel like to *set up* Web4 for the first time. The mockups show the end state beautifully, but the first-run experience is a gap.
- **Some pages are long**: Why Web4, Coherence Index, and Aliveness are thorough to the point where a less patient visitor might bounce. The content is all valuable, but the scroll depth is significant.

**Overall**: I came in knowing nothing about Web4 and left with a solid mental model of the entire system. The site achieved its educational goal. The combination of concrete mockups, interactive simulations, honest limitations, and progressive complexity is rare and well-executed. I'd recommend it to anyone interested in trust, identity, or internet governance.
