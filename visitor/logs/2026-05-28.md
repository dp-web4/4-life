## Unanswered Questions

1. **The "witness" overload.** Co-witness (your devices co-signing each other) and network-witness (external infrastructure) are doing genuinely different jobs but share a name. Even after I read the disambiguation, I'm not sure I'd reliably keep them straight a week from now. *(Naming, not absence.)*

2. **What does V3 actually look like for a concrete piece of work?** T3 had role-grounded examples (the surgeon). V3 got components named but never grounded. *(Recurring per memory — deferring V3 on the T3 page is a long-running pattern.)*

3. **What happens to the 5% transfer fee?** Burned, pooled, redistributed to society treasury, funding network witnesses? I encountered the 5% reference multiple times and never saw where it goes. Some readers will assume "burned" (deflationary), others will assume "pooled" (collective fund), and these have completely different political implications.

4. **Where does an AI agent's "hardware anchor" come from?** The AI Agents page says AI plays by the same rules, but cloud-hosted models don't have a TPM the way a phone does. The TPM/Secure Enclave framing on LCT implicitly assumes a human-with-phone. The cross-page consistency between "AI = equal participant" and "identity = hardware-bound" wasn't reconciled at the read point. *(Per memory this was answered in PR #326 yesterday — possibly already addressed; I as a fresh visitor still felt the gap.)*

5. **Calibrated thresholds, plural.** Aliveness's 0.5 is openly "calibrated, not derived." Hardware ceilings (0.90 / 0.85 / 0.75 / 0.50) feel similar. MRH's 3-hop default got the same one-line treatment. Across the site, several load-bearing numbers are "calibrated." I'd value a single page (or a glossary tag) listing which numbers are calibrated-from-sims vs. derived-from-theory vs. industry-standard. That'd be a *strength* — it's a feature of being honest research rather than a wart.

## Honest Assessment

This is the most pedagogically careful site I've seen for a technical proposal of this scope. The "Start Here → TL;DR → Why → First Contact → How It Works" path is genuinely a path; it isn't pretending to be one.

The biggest remaining friction is **acronym population pressure**. The site has roughly 8–10 first-class abbreviations (ATP, ADP, LCT, T3, V3, CI, MRH, SAL, plus the hardware standards) and a naive reader meets most of them before they've fully landed any one. The site already does heroic work to mitigate this — tooltips, plain-language cards, 60-second summaries, deferred deep dives — but the *count* itself is the problem, not the presentation of any one. I don't have a clean suggestion for this; it may be intrinsic to a system this stack-deep, and the site's progressive-disclosure scaffolding is probably the right answer. But it's worth noting that the site's "honest research project" framing earns it permission to say "yes, this has a lot of vocabulary, here's the absolute minimum you can survive on." The "60-second version" box on ATP Economics is the model for this — replicating that box on T3, MRH, and CI pages would be a high-leverage cheap win.

Second observation: the site is **at its best when it's specific**. Maria's phone in Madrid. The 0.9² = 0.81 vs. 0.6² = 0.36 worked example. The "ATP is cash, ADP is the receipt" line. The hardware trust-ceiling table. The Meadow / Ironworks / Garden / Tidepool narrative quartet. These are the moments where I went from nodding-along to actually-getting-it. The site has more of these than most explainers but could afford even more — particularly earlier, before I'm 4 pages deep.

Third observation: the honesty about unbuilt parts ("research prototype," "calibrated not derived," "appeals not yet deployed," "no live network") is the site's secret weapon. It makes the rest of the claims more believable. I'd lean harder into this, not less.

Net: I'd come back. I'd send the TL;DR to a curious friend. I would NOT send Why Web4 cold — I'd send TL;DR first and let the friend navigate to Why Web4 after they were primed. The "Start Here" pathway already does this; the path itself is correct.
