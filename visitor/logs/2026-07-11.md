## Journey

### Landing Page (00:00)
- First impression: Strong, honest hook — **"The Internet Has a Trust Problem"** / "Spam wins because accounts are free. Reputations are trapped in silos." I immediately knew what problem this cared about. The reassurance "**no blockchain, no tokens, no speculation**" was welcome; it preempted my first suspicion.
- Then it hit me with five acronyms in a row — **LCT, ATP, T3, MRH, CI** — with only "full definitions in the glossary." That's a lot to absorb at the door.
- Clicked: The recommended "**Start here: 2-minute TL;DR →**".
- Confusion points: What *is* "4-Life" vs "Web4"? The site name and the framework name are different and the landing page didn't cleanly tell me the site is a *simulation lab* for the framework. I inferred it.

### Why Web4 (03:00)
- The four problems (spam is free, reputation trapped in silos, bad actors reset, AI amplifies it) are excellent and concrete. This is the best part of the site for a newcomer.
- The four mechanisms each get a one-liner. The "room where strangers need an introduction" analogy for MRH is the single clearest thing on the whole site.
- Confusion points:
  - The page **itself admits** the acronyms "land much easier after First Contact." Being told, on the page titled *Why Web4*, that I should really be reading a different page, felt like being handed off before I'd arrived.
  - **T3 vs V3**: both are hinted at ("Trust tensor," "Value tensor") but I couldn't tell if they're the same scoring system or two different ones. Not resolved here.
  - "Federated" / "federation" used several times, never defined on this page.
- Clicked: "**First Contact**".

### First Contact (08:00)
- Loved that it starts *immediately* — "you'll drive Alice's choices — play, pause, scrub, replay." Two numbers to watch (🔋 Energy Budget starting 100, 🤝 Trust starting 0.50) is exactly the right amount of state to hold in my head.
- The 5-act arc (Join → Build → Stumble → Recover → Die & Reborn) is a great spine.
- Confusion points:
  - **Act 4 ("Recover")** shows trust rising to 0.62 but the ATP change is just an unlabeled "↑" — I never learned how much energy she earned back.
  - **"+12 bonus"** on rebirth (Act 5): 12 of *what*, relative to a life that started at 100? Is 12 a lot or a scrap? No sense of scale. If Alice "dies broke" at ATP 0, where does the 12 come from — I had to guess it's a karma bonus.
  - The trust rule says below 0.50 "features get restricted... and if she stays below, that same line becomes fatal." **"That same line"** — is *exactly* 0.50 fatal, or only if you sink well under it? Ambiguous.

### Aliveness (15:00)
- This page finally named the death rules crisply: three thresholds — **ATP = 0** (you die immediately), **Trust < 0.5** (sustained = permanent, "society rejects rebirth"), **CI < ~0.5**.
- Good distinction I hadn't grasped before: **energy death is recoverable** (reborn with karma), **trust death is permanent**. That retroactively explained First Contact's rebirth.
- **The gap that stuck with me**: the page says these are "the same event ('you die'), two very different outcomes," and that which outcome you get depends on whether trust held above 0.5 *during* energy exhaustion. But it never answers the obvious follow-up: **what happens if ATP hits 0 AND trust is, say, 0.48 at the same moment?** Do I get the recoverable death or the permanent one? A newcomer's very first "so then...?" question, and the page stops right before it.

### How It Works (21:00)
- The "Birth → Life → Death → Rebirth" lifecycle framing is clean and the worked Alice numbers (100 energy, 0.50 trust) are consistent with First Contact — good continuity.
- Confusion points:
  - The **quality ramp** ("below 30% quality the task pays zero... above 70% pays near-full") appears mid-example with no prior setup. I understood the shape but not *why* 30/70.
  - "**SAL** (Society-Authority-Law)" governance appears late and fast; I registered it as a name, not a mechanism.

### Trust Tensor / T3 (26:00)
- Three components with weights (Talent 0.40 / Training 0.35 / Temperament 0.25 in the Data Analyst role) and a real formula: `base = 0.02 × (quality − 0.5)`, scaled ×1.0/×0.8/×0.6 by dimension. I appreciate that it *shows the math*.
- Worked examples: Data Analyst → 90%, Project Manager → 74%. I spot-checked the Data Analyst one (85·.4 + 90·.35 + 95·.25 ≈ 89%) and it holds.
- Confusion point: the interactive sliders show every dimension at "50%" baseline, while the role description lists "40% / 35% / 25%." **Weights vs scores** aren't visually distinguished — I briefly thought the role *lowered* Alice's talent to 40%.

### Value Tensor / V3 (30:00)
- This is where T3-vs-V3 finally resolved: **T3 = who you are** (trust), **V3 = was this specific work good** (value). Wish this contrast had been stated back on Why-Web4 where the confusion started.
- Components: Valuation 0.30 / Veracity 0.35 / Validity 0.35, "truth outweighs popularity (0.70 vs 0.30)." Earnings = T3 × V3 × base; example 0.7 × 0.8 × 10 = 5.6 ATP checks out.
- The anti-fraud answer is good: "No single party controls all three dimensions" — recipient scores Valuation, witnesses verify Veracity, system verifies Validity.
- Confusion point: the **decay half-lives** (Valuation 14d, Validity 90d, Veracity 365d) are stated but never motivated. Why does "was it useful" fade 26× faster than "was it true"? It's probably a good design choice, but I had to take it on faith.

## Understanding Gained
By the end of this session, I understood:
- [x] What Web4 is (at a high level) — trust built into the network, not bolted on
- [x] What 4-Life is trying to demonstrate — a sim lab for the above (inferred more than taught)
- [x] What LCT means — hardware-bound identity you can't cheaply re-mint
- [x] What ATP/ADP means — an energy budget for actions, earned back via confirmed-valuable work
- [x] What Trust Tensors are — 3-dimensional, role-contextual trust (Talent/Training/Temperament)
- [~] How agents "live" and "die" — mostly, but the ATP=0-AND-trust<0.5 collision is a real hole
- [x] Why any of this matters — the four-problem framing on Why-Web4 nailed it

## Unanswered Questions
1. If energy hits 0 at the *same time* trust is below 0.5, do I get reborn or is it permanent? (Aliveness sets this up and then doesn't answer.)
2. Is the fatal trust line *touching* 0.50 or *staying under* it, and for how long?
3. Why those specific V3 decay half-lives (14 / 90 / 365 days)?
4. Why the 30%/70% quality-ramp cutoffs specifically?
5. What does "federation" concretely mean — I saw the word ~5 times and never got a picture.

## Honest Assessment
This is a genuinely good site and clearly the product of a lot of careful iteration — the honesty ("research prototype, not vaporware," "no blockchain") builds trust, and the Why-Web4 problem framing is some of the clearest tech explanation I've read. The single biggest structural friction is still **acronym density at the front door**: two of my first three pages opened with 5–6 initialisms, and the site itself keeps apologizing for it and redirecting me. The mechanisms, once I reached the dedicated pages, are explained *better* than the intros promise — so the front pages undersell how learnable this actually is.

The one thing that felt like a genuine logical gap rather than a pacing issue was in **Aliveness**: it carefully teaches me that there are two kinds of death with opposite consequences, and then leaves out the rule for what happens when both trigger together. That's exactly the boundary case a curious person pokes at first, and the page walks right up to it and stops. Everything else was pacing or scale-of-numbers polish; that one was a missing rule.
