## Unanswered Questions
1. **What does the actual UI look like in practice?** The Day in Web4 page has mockups, but I'm curious about real interface prototypes — wireframes, interaction flows. The app mockup URLs (mail.web4.local, talent.web4.local) are tantalizing but imaginary.
2. **How does MRH actually work in practice?** It's described as "trust neighborhoods" and "strangers need introductions" but I didn't visit the dedicated MRH page. Of the six core concepts, this one got the least explanation in the pages I visited.
3. **What happens during the transition period?** If Web4 starts as a browser extension (Tier 1), how do trust scores work when most people on a platform aren't using it? Does a 1% adoption rate produce useful trust signals?
4. **How do you handle context collapse?** My Trust Tensor as a data analyst is separate from my trust as a community member — but what if I'm doing data analysis for my community? Role boundaries seem clean in theory but messy in practice.

## Honest Assessment

This is an impressively well-constructed educational site. The progression from "The Internet Has a Trust Problem" through concrete scenarios to interactive simulations is thoughtful and effective. I went from zero Web4 knowledge to genuinely understanding the core concepts in about 40 minutes.

**What works exceptionally well:**
- The TL;DR page is a perfect entry point — it should be pushed even harder
- "A Day in Web4" is the most compelling page because it makes abstract concepts concrete
- Consistent honesty about limitations ("this is research," "no timeline commitments") builds genuine credibility
- The concept sequence (LCT→ATP→T3→MRH→CI→Aliveness) provides a clear learning path
- Interactive elements (Playground experiments, Trust Tensor simulator, Coherence Index sliders) transform passive reading into active learning
- The site respects my time — time estimates on buttons, progressive disclosure, collapsible sections

**What could be better:**
- The Aliveness page is a masterclass in content but needs a summary at the top — it's the longest page and comes at the end of the concept sequence when visitor energy is lowest
- MRH (trust neighborhoods) feels like the least-integrated concept — it's in the sequence but doesn't feature prominently in the landing page or the "four ideas" framing on Why Web4
- The footer date (Apr 11) doesn't match the latest What's New entry (Apr 12) — small but it undermines the "actively maintained" message

**Overall**: This is one of the clearest explanations of a complex technical system I've encountered. The site teaches rather than lectures, which is rare. I would return to explore the interactive simulations more deeply, and I'd recommend it to anyone curious about internet trust infrastructure.
