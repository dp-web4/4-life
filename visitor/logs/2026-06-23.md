## Honest Assessment
This is a genuinely well-built explainer, and several things I'd have flagged in the past are visibly fixed. The landing page front door is unambiguous, First Contact remains the best teaching surface on the site, the Karma Journey now leads with a raw-vs-effective anchor that defused my "did I already fail" panic, and the death distinction (energy = recoverable, trust = permanent) is one of the clearest ideas on the site.

The two things that still cost me real effort were both about *density at the wrong depth*, not absence of explanation:

1. **The "what's live" page assumes vocabulary it doesn't supply.** The new orientation banner is a real improvement and correctly warns me — but warning isn't teaching. Even right after reading the TL;DR I hit "witness chain / sealed channels / V3 / MCP server" with no inline help. The banner steers newcomers away; it doesn't catch the ones who stay. If this keeps coming up, the fix probably isn't more banner wording — it's a couple of inline glosses or concept-page links on the jargon itself.

2. **The Karma Journey's deep math block.** The *intro* anchor is good. The *deep* "0.85² ≈ 0.72 → raw ≈ 0.97 near-impossible" passage is where I stalled and re-read. It's the kind of thing that wants to be behind an opt-in "show the math" reveal rather than sitting in the main reading flow.

Neither was a dealbreaker — I'd return, and I left understanding Web4 well. But both are exactly the residual friction that survives after the obvious fixes have landed: the explanation exists, it's just sitting at a depth or in a density that a linear naive reader hits at the wrong moment.
