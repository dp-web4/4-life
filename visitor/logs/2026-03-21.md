## Honest Assessment

This is genuinely one of the most well-constructed educational sites I've encountered for a complex technical concept. The progressive disclosure works beautifully — I could get the 2-minute version, the 7-minute version, or spend an hour going deep, and each level felt complete rather than truncated.

The strongest pages are the **landing page** (clear problem framing), **Day in Web4** (makes it tangible), **What Could Go Wrong** (builds trust through honesty), and **First Contact** (Alice's story teaches through narrative). The concept sequence (LCT→ATP→T3→MRH→CI→Aliveness) is logical and each concept genuinely builds on the previous one.

The site has an enormous amount of content (77 pages!) but it doesn't feel overwhelming because the learning paths are well-curated. I could see a visitor spending 10 minutes or 2 hours and feeling satisfied either way.

My main takeaway: Web4 proposes that if you make identity hardware-bound, actions energy-costed, trust multi-dimensional, and consequences permanent, you get a self-regulating internet where spam dies naturally and quality compounds. The simulations on this site are compelling evidence that the math works. Whether it works with real humans remains the open question — and the site is honest about that.

The "Day in Web4" page with its five app mockups (Mail, Talent, Reviews, Social, Market) is what made it click for me. Every concept page explains the *mechanism*, but that page shows the *experience*. I'd put it much earlier in the visitor journey.
