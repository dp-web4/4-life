## Unanswered Questions

1. **What exactly is ADP?** ATP is explained well (energy budget, action costs). But ADP — what is it? The biological metaphor implies ATP→ADP is energy spent, but the site barely mentions ADP by name. Is it just "spent ATP"? Does it convert back?
2. **How do LCTs actually work?** I know devices have security chips and they're "linked together" through LCTs, but what's the actual linking mechanism? Is it a cryptographic protocol? A certificate chain? A handshake?
3. **What constitutes a "society"?** Is it a platform? A community? A Discord server? The site uses the term heavily but never clearly defines the boundary.
4. **How does federation work between societies with different trust standards?** The adoption path mentions "Federated" as a phase, and the What Could Go Wrong page mentions cross-society conflicts, but the mechanism for federation is murky.
5. **Who runs the witness network?** Witnesses are mentioned for identity recovery and bootstrapping, but who are they? How do they get their role? Is there an incentive structure?
6. **What's the actual privacy model?** MRH limits visibility, and zero-knowledge proofs are mentioned, but can someone reconstruct your full activity by aggregating what different people in your network see?
7. **Why "Markov" in MRH?** The connection to Markov processes/chains is never explained.

## Honest Assessment

This is one of the better-structured research project websites I've seen. The progression from "2-minute TL;DR" to "deep interactive tools" is well thought out, and the intellectual honesty (especially the "What Could Go Wrong" page) builds genuine credibility.

**What worked really well:**
- The landing page hooks you immediately with a relatable problem (spam, reputation silos, AI deception)
- The TL;DR genuinely delivers on its promise — I understood the core idea in 2 minutes
- Alice's First Contact story is the single best onboarding tool — concrete numbers, cause and effect, a full lifecycle
- "A Day in Web4" grounds everything in relatable daily scenarios
- The "What Could Go Wrong" page is a masterclass in building trust through transparency

**What needs work:**
- **Two core concepts have no dedicated explainer pages**: Attention economics (ATP/ADP) and Linked Context Tokens (LCT) both return 404. These are foundational pillars referenced on nearly every page — they need their own deep dives.
- **ADP is almost invisible**: The landing page formula mentions ATP/ADP, but ADP itself is barely explained anywhere. The biological metaphor (ATP energy → ADP spent energy) is implicit but never made explicit.
- **The interactive tools are the weakest link for text-based browsing**: The Society Simulator, Playground, Karma Journey, and Trust Dilemmas all require JavaScript interaction. For a text-only visitor (or someone with JS disabled), these pages feel like promises rather than experiences.
- **Math opacity**: The Coherence Index formula, karma carry-forward calculations, and MRH cost multipliers are presented as results ("0.85 retains 72%") without showing the underlying math. For a curious visitor who wants to verify or understand, this is frustrating.

**Overall**: The site successfully explains *why* Web4 matters and *what* it proposes within the first 5-10 minutes. The interactive tools and deeper concept pages extend understanding for those who want to invest more time. The honest acknowledgment of unsolved problems is its strongest trust-building feature. I would return, and I would recommend this to someone interested in internet trust, identity, or governance.

The two HIGH-severity 404s on core concept pages (attention economics and LCT) are the most impactful fixes — these are the concepts visitors will most want to explore after reading the overview pages.
