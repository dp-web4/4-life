## Unanswered Questions
1. **What's the realistic timeline?** The site says "active research" and "not deployed." Is this 2 years from a pilot? 10 years? 50? Even a rough range would help calibrate expectations.
2. **Who is actually working on this?** The "Who is building this?" FAQ says "open research project" but gives no sense of team size, institutional backing, or how many people are actively contributing. For a project asking me to take it seriously, some transparency about the team would help.
3. **What does success look like?** There's no roadmap or milestones page. What would a successful pilot look like? What's the first real-world deployment target? A "Next Steps" section would help visitors understand the trajectory.
4. **How would existing platforms integrate?** The 5-tier adoption model is described well in theory, but I'd love to see a concrete "Here's how Discord/Reddit/Stack Overflow could add Tier 1" walkthrough — even hypothetical.

## Honest Assessment

This is one of the most impressive educational/research sites I've browsed. The level of intellectual honesty is remarkable — every section has "Honest caveat" or "What's genuinely unsolved" admissions that actually build trust in the project rather than undermining it. The self-aware irony of a trust-focused project modeling transparency is not lost on me.

**What works exceptionally well:**
- The progressive disclosure architecture. I could get the 2-minute version OR spend an hour in deep FAQs — my choice.
- The "Day in Web4" wireframes are the single most compelling content on the site. They make the abstract concrete. These should be more prominent.
- The concept sequence (LCT → ATP → T3 → MRH → CI → Aliveness) builds knowledge incrementally and each page references the others.
- The interactive elements (Karma Journey, Trust Tensor simulator, CI sliders, Society Simulator) let me develop intuition rather than just read theory.
- The "What Could Go Wrong" page. Publishing your own failure analysis is the most trust-building thing on the site.

**What could be improved:**
- The site is very good at explaining *what* Web4 is and *why* it matters, but light on *when* and *who*. There's no roadmap, no team page, no timeline. For a project asking visitors to take it seriously, some sense of momentum and trajectory would help.
- The wireframes in "Day in Web4" are buried. They should be teased on the landing page — they're the best "aha moment" content.
- A "What's New" or changelog would help returning visitors. The "Last update: Mar 19" footer is good but doesn't tell me what changed.

**Overall:** I came in skeptical ("another blockchain-adjacent thing?") and left genuinely impressed. The ideas are well-reasoned, the honesty is refreshing, and the simulations make abstract concepts tangible. The main gap isn't explanation quality — it's the absence of forward-looking information about what happens next. I understand *what* Web4 proposes; I want to know *where* it's going.
