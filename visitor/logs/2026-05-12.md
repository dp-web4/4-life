## Unanswered Questions

1. **Who actually measures Talent/Training/Temperament dimensions?** I got the answer (recipient attestation) only by navigating to How It Works. The Trust Tensor page should answer this directly.

2. **"Resources actually present in the society"** — what does this mean concretely? Compute? Human labor? In simulation this can be hand-waved but as a naive visitor I want one tangible example.

3. **Cold-start problem at scale**: if a small group uses Web4, how do they recharge ATP without enough peer recipients? The 100 ATP starter grant runs out — what happens before there's a community big enough to attest?

4. **Privacy tension**: If my reputation is portable across platforms via LCT, does that mean every platform sees my full behavior history? Or is there selective disclosure? (Saw a glossary mention of ZK proofs but didn't follow through.)

5. **Real vs. simulated rebirth**: The Aliveness page clarifies this is "license suspension" in real deployment, but Karma Journey leans hard into multi-life rebirth narrative. A visitor who plays Karma Journey first might be confused about the mapping.

## Honest Assessment

This is a genuinely well-crafted educational site for a complex topic. The pedagogy is layered — TL;DR for the impatient, First Contact for the experiential, How It Works for the systematic, Day in Web4 for the imaginative, What Could Go Wrong for the skeptical. The candor on Page "What Could Go Wrong" — admitting that 3 billion people don't have smartphones, that witness governance is unsolved, that adoption is the existential risk — is what made me trust the rest.

The biggest residual friction is **acronym sequencing**. Even with plain-English labels, having 5-7 acronyms cluster on entry pages forces context-switching at exactly the moment a visitor is trying to absorb the core idea. The site has clearly worked hard on this (I can see TermTooltips, glossary, expansion patterns) but Why Web4's solutions section in particular still piles them up.

The other recurring miss is **answers buried on the wrong page**: who measures T3 dimensions is on How It Works, not Trust Tensor. The "rebirth maps to license suspension" reconciliation is on Aliveness, not Karma Journey. A visitor who clicks into Trust Tensor or Karma Journey directly (likely if they share a deep link) lands without these answers.

Best pages: Day in Web4 (concrete daily-life examples), What Could Go Wrong (candid self-critique), Coherence Index (Maria's stolen phone example), LCT Explainer (driver's license analogy + device witnessing).

Weakest moments: the "anchored to witnessed measurement of resources" phrasing on ATP page, the unanswered "who measures T3?" on Trust Tensor, and the lingering "Markov" intimidation on MRH.

Net: I would return. I'd recommend it to a curious technical friend, with caveat that they start at the TL;DR and read Day in Web4 second.
