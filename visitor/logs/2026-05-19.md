## Journey

### Landing Page (00:00)
- First impression: Clean, calm, and unusually honest. The headline "4-Life: A Trust-Native Internet Lab" plus the parenthetical "Web4: a proposed internet trust model — trust verified by behavior, not assumed by default; no blockchain, not a Web3 successor" answered my three biggest reflexive questions (What is this? Is it crypto? Is it a Web3 thing?) before I could even ask them. That's rare and it lowered my guard immediately.
- "The Internet Has a Trust Problem" landed — spam is free, reputation is siloed, bad actors reset. I recognized all three from my own life.
- The "Not a product, not a company, not a blockchain... Right now, you can't install it" line is disarming. I trust a site more when it tells me what it *can't* do.
- Clicked: "Start here: 2-minute TL;DR" (the recommended path — I followed the site's own advice).
- Confusion points: A small cluster of acronyms (LCT, ATP, T3, MRH, CI) is named on the landing page. They're each given a plain-English partner word (Identity, Energy, Trust, Neighborhood, Consistency), which softened it. Not friction yet — just noted that the alphabet soup is visible early.

### TL;DR (03:00)
- This is exactly what I wanted. Problem → Core Idea → Result, in plain language. The ATP biology aside ("ATP is the molecule your cells spend to do work") finally explained *why* this thing has a chemistry name — that had been nagging me on the landing page and here it was answered in one clause. Good.
- "Identity anchors to device hardware (LCT) — making fake identities expensive" is the cleanest one-sentence version of the idea I'd seen.
- T3/MRH/CI named but deferred. Fine for a 2-minute page — it set expectations honestly.
- Clicked: "First Contact →".

### First Contact (06:00)
- The reality-status framing was clear and I appreciated it: "Real rules, shown by simulation — not a demo of a shipped product" and "you're running a simulation of the proposed model, not using a product." Last time something like this might have left me wondering "wait, is this real or pretend?" — here it was stated plainly twice. No dissonance.

- Confusion: the four concepts (ATP/T3/LCT/CI) are name-dropped briefly before the walkthrough. "Hardware-bound" and "coherence" weren't really explained before being used. Mild — the walkthrough promises to explain as it goes, so I gave it benefit of the doubt.
- Clicked: "Why Web4?" (I wanted the *why* before going deeper into the *what*).

### Why Web4? (12:00)
- The five problems each get a "root cause" then "why past fixes (moderation, CAPTCHA, Web3 wallets, social login) missed it." This is a genuinely good argument structure — it earns the solution instead of asserting it.
- The analogies (party introduction, phone minutes on a shared plan, email federation, academic transcript) are well chosen.
- Friction: this page is **long for a newcomer**. The four mechanisms get only summary-level treatment with "learn more →" links, and then there's a 120+ question FAQ. I could feel the depth but the four mechanisms themselves deserve a compact visual or step-through *before* the FAQ, rather than "here's a summary, go read other pages." The FAQ index also sits midway down — I wouldn't have known 120+ Q&As existed if I hadn't scrolled.
- Clicked: "Make trust choices →" (Karma Journey).

### Karma Journey (18:00)
- The reincarnation framing ("Live multiple lives... trust and karma compound across reincarnations") is evocative and the nine-action menu is concrete.
- **Biggest friction of the session here.** Two things stacked:
  1. **Raw trust vs. effective trust.** Raw trust keeps you alive *now*; effective trust (= raw × CI²) shapes what you inherit *next life*. I didn't grasp why I should care about effective trust if raw is what keeps me breathing. The distinction is presented as fact before it's motivated.
  2. **The CI² mechanic and the "1.4x cost multiplier."** I start at CI 0.85 (not 1.0) "because you lack history," which makes a 10-ATP action cost 14. This is shown as arithmetic. The justification for *squaring* CI ("small dips forgiven, sustained dips punished") arrives *after* I've already been hit by the math — I learned the rule backwards. "You're new, so it costs more" tells me the *what*, not the *why*.

## Understanding Gained
By the end of this session, I understood:
- [x] What Web4 is (at a high level) — trust verified by behavior, device-anchored identity, energy-budgeted actions
- [x] What 4-Life is trying to demonstrate — an interactive lab/simulation of the proposed model
- [x] What LCT means — hardware-bound identity, "phone as an unforgeable driver's license"
- [x] What ATP/ADP means — energy budget you spend and recharge via validated work; ADP is the receipt

## Unanswered Questions
1. **Why is the Coherence Index *squared*?** I saw `× CI²` and `1 / CI²` on two pages and never got an intuition for the squaring specifically — just that it's "less forgiving."
2. **Why do I, a brand-new participant, start penalized (CI 0.85, 1.4x cost)?** I understand I have no history, but the leap from "no history" to "you pay 40% more" wasn't motivated. It feels like the system distrusts me before I've done anything.
3. **What are MRH and V3?** The Society Simulator used both as if I'd know them; nothing on the page told me.
4. **Is there a single "right" path through this site?** I followed the site's pointers and still felt like I might be doing it out of order.

## Honest Assessment
This is a genuinely well-built explanatory site, and it has clearly improved — the reality-status framing on First Contact was unambiguous (no "is this real or pretend?" wobble this time), the ATP definition is consistent and grounded, and the Aliveness page's energy-death-vs-trust-death distinction is now crisp. The LCT driver's-license analogy and the TL;DR are standout pieces of writing.

The one thing that nagged me most was structural rather than cosmetic: **the same CI² math showed up unexplained on two different pages (Karma Journey and Aliveness), and both times the page handed me a formula before giving me a reason to believe it.** When the *same* gap appears in two places independently, it stops being a per-page nitpick and starts being the thing to fix — ideally with one shared, plain-English "why squared, why now" explanation that both pages reuse. Tied to that is the "why does a newcomer start penalized" question — emotionally, being told I cost 40% more before I've acted reads as the system distrusting me by default, which is ironically the opposite of the "trust earned by behavior, not assumed" pitch the landing page made so well. Closing that loop ("you're not being punished — you just haven't been *witnessed* yet, and here's how fast that changes") would resolve the single most resonant confusion of the session.

Net: solid understanding, would return, would recommend. The site taught me; it didn't make me infer. The remaining friction is concentrated, recurring, and very fixable.
