## Unanswered Questions
1. **What does actual deployment look like?** The Tier 1 "browser extension wrapper" concept is mentioned but there's no prototype, mockup, or timeline for when anyone could actually use this. The Day in Web4 mockups are conceptual; a "here's what a Tier 1 Reddit extension would actually look like" demo would help.
2. **How does the system handle legitimate multi-accounting?** Many people have separate personal and professional identities online. If identity is hardware-bound, can one person have multiple legitimate personas on the same device?
3. **What happens during the transition period?** If only 5% of platforms adopt Web4, my "portable reputation" is only portable to those 5%. The adoption tiers address this somewhat, but the practical experience during the long middle period between "nothing" and "everything" is unclear.
4. **How does trust transfer work across radically different communities?** My trust as a data analyst traveling to a cooking community — the site says it's role-scoped, but does my *temperament* (reliability) transfer? The federation economics page might address this but I didn't visit it.

## Honest Assessment

This is one of the most thoroughly constructed educational sites I've encountered for a technical research project. The site consistently follows its own principles: problem before solution, plain English before jargon, concrete before abstract.

**What works exceptionally well:**
- The multiple entry points by time commitment (2 min TL;DR, 3 min Your Internet, 5 min Why Web4, 7 min First Contact) respect different visitor patience levels
- The Day in Web4 page with app mockups makes the abstract vision concrete — this was the single most effective page for understanding
- The "What Could Go Wrong" page is remarkable in its honesty — admitting "nobody adopts it" as the #1 existential risk builds enormous credibility
- The concept sequence (LCT → ATP → T3 → MRH → CI → Aliveness) creates a logical progression where each piece builds on the last
- Interactive elements on almost every concept page (sliders, simulators, scenarios) make passive reading active

**What could be improved:**
- The Karma Journey's initial effective trust display (0.361) creates momentary confusion when the 0.5 threshold has been drilled in as the life/death line — the distinction between raw trust and effective trust needs to be introduced more gently at the point of first encounter
- The sheer volume of content, while impressive, can feel overwhelming — even with good progressive disclosure, 20 pages deep I still hadn't visited the Federation Economics, Threat Model, or several other pages

**Overall impression:** The site successfully taught me a complex system through progressive disclosure. I came in knowing nothing about Web4 and left with a solid understanding of the core mechanics, genuine appreciation for the design tensions, and respect for the intellectual honesty. The combination of rigorous technical depth and accessible plain-English explanation is rare and effective. I would return to explore the interactive labs more deeply.
