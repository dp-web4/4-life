## Unanswered Questions

1. **Is this for humans, AI, or both?** The landing page says "AI and humans coexist" but the simulation focuses on "agents." Would I, as a human user, have an ATP budget and Trust Tensor?

2. **How does hardware-bound identity work in practice?** Do I need a specific device? What happens when I get a new phone or laptop? The explainer mentions "witness networks" but that's still abstract.

3. **Is this blockchain/crypto or not?** Web4 sounds like a sequel to Web3. The site never directly addresses "is this another crypto thing?" which is what many visitors will wonder.

4. **What's the "First Contact" tutorial supposed to show?** The page mentions Alice and a 10-minute experience but I couldn't find actual interactive content.

5. **How does this connect to real applications?** The simulation is interesting but I don't understand how I would actually USE Web4 for anything practical.

6. **Who is building this and who would adopt it?** Is this academic research? A startup? An open protocol? Is anyone actually implementing Web4 systems?

## Honest Assessment

This site has a **genuinely interesting idea** buried under **layers of conceptual overhead**. The core insight — that making actions cost something and tying identity to hardware could naturally eliminate spam and bad actors — is compelling. The biological metaphor (ATP, metabolism, life/death) is creative and helps once you understand it.

But the onboarding experience expects too much from visitors. I had to visit 8+ pages to piece together what this is about. The landing page throws jargon without definitions. The "First Contact" tutorial promises an interactive experience I couldn't find. The Playground lets me adjust parameters without understanding what they mean.

**What the site does well:**
- The "Why Web4" problem framing resonates — spam, siloed reputation, and AI deception are real issues
- The Glossary is comprehensive once you find it
- Individual explainer pages (ATP Economics, Trust Tensor, Aliveness) are quite clear
- The biological metaphor is memorable and internally consistent

**What needs work:**
- Lead with the problem, not the jargon. Show me WHY I should care before showing me WHAT the solution is.
- Progressive disclosure — don't dump four acronyms on me immediately
- Make the Playground usable without prior knowledge — add context and tooltips
- Clarify the audience — is this for AI researchers, developers, regular people, or investors?
- Answer the obvious skeptical questions ("is this crypto?", "who's building this?", "why should I trust this?")

**Bottom line:** I'm intellectually curious but not hooked. I understand the concept better after 25 minutes of exploration, but I still don't know what I would DO with this knowledge or whether it's something that will ever affect me. The site assumes I'm already bought in and just need details — but it hasn't sold me yet.
