## Unanswered Questions
1. **What happens to a brand-new participant at trust 0.50 who makes one mistake?** Is that permanent death? This is the question I most wanted answered and three pages each gave me a third of a contradiction.
2. If ATP transfers exist, what stops an off-platform cash market for ATP ("I PayPal you $20, you transfer me 100 ATP")?
3. Why is "tensor" the right word for T3? Everything I saw was three numbers averaged.
4. Who runs the witnesses on day one, and what do they get out of it? (Aliveness says 3 witnesses must attest a birth; the economics of being a witness stayed vague.)
5. What is a visitor who's convinced supposed to *do*? The site argues well and offers no on-ramp beyond reading more.

## Honest Assessment
This is the most intellectually honest project site I can remember visiting. "What Could Go Wrong" leading with "nobody adopts it" as the #1 risk, the settled-vs-evolving split on ATP economics, and the confidence labels ("designed but untested") all earned trust that the persuasive pages then spent wisely. The pedagogical spine — problem first (/why-web4), one character and two numbers (/first-contact), then deepening layers — is sound.

The site's remaining weakness isn't any single page; it's **cross-page consistency**. Each page is internally careful, but the pages disagree with each other at the joints: LCT means different things on /tldr and /lct-explainer, the karma tiers on /karma-journey look impossible under /aliveness's death rules until you derive the raw-vs-effective distinction yourself, and — most importantly — the 0.50 starting trust and the 0.5 permadeath line are stated on different pages and never reconciled anywhere. A visitor reading the whole site is *more* confused about newcomer survival than a visitor reading one page, which is backwards.

The acronym problem, which I gather is endemic to this material, is now mostly managed — every page offers plain-English handles and glossary escapes, and only MRH and the surprise "V3" actually stung. What's left is harder than vocabulary: making five careful pages tell one consistent story.

I'd come back. Mostly because I want to know whether the newcomer at 0.50 lives.
