## Journey

### Landing Page (00:00)
- First impression: "The Internet Has a Trust Problem" — clear hook. I immediately understood the three complaints (spam is free, reputation is siloed, bad actors get infinite fresh starts) because I've lived all three.
- The five core ideas row (Identity/Energy/Trust/Neighborhood/Consistency) with plain words *before* the acronyms (LCT, ATP, T3, MRH, CI) worked. I didn't retain the acronyms yet, but I wasn't scared off.
- "Not a product, not a company, not a blockchain" — I appreciated this. My first reflex on seeing "Web4" was "crypto thing"; the page defused that in the first screen.
- Clicked: the recommended "2-minute TL;DR".
- Confusion points: the "What's New" panel's latest entry is Jun 11 — over a month old. Minor, but on a page that says things are actively developed, I noticed.

### Web4 in 2 Minutes (04:00)
- Genuinely about 2 minutes. The four principles (actions cost energy, identity via hardware, trust is earned, same rules for humans and AI) stuck with me.
- "Constellation" of devices was a new word introduced without ceremony, but the meaning was guessable.
- One line referenced "what 'death' means here" — first time death was mentioned, no explanation offered on this page. It read as a teaser but left me slightly uneasy: my *identity* can die?
- Also filed away a skeptical question the page didn't answer: trust is earned when others confirm my work — who confirms *their* trustworthiness at the very beginning?
- Clicked: First Contact (the recommended next step).

### First Contact (07:00)
- The Alice walkthrough is the best onboarding piece on the site. Two numbers to watch (Energy starts at 100, Trust starts at 0.50), five acts, and the system pauses to explain itself at turning points. After the spam stumble (−25 ATP, trust 0.50→0.48) I understood the whole economic loop viscerally.
- The upfront banner "this is a browser simulation of a *proposed* system, not a deployed product" earned trust with me.
- Confusion points: Alice's rebirth comes with "+12 karma bonus" — the number appears without any hint of how it was computed. I accepted it as "reputation carried forward" but couldn't say why 12 and not 30.
- "Death" still felt jarring even after the spoiler note softened it to "standing suspended, history persists."

### Why Web4? (16:00)
- Longest read of the session and the most impressive. The FAQ answers questions I actually had, including hostile ones ("what if a powerful person lies about a newcomer?", "can I buy 50 devices for high trust?", "is 'low quality' just censorship with extra steps?").
- The honesty is unusual: "no timeline — and that's intentional", "unresolved policy question" (GDPR), "the gap to billions contains unknown challenges". I trust the authors *more* because of these admissions, while trusting the *feasibility* less.
- Confusion points: V3 appears late ("rate how good each specific thing you make is") and its relationship to T3 wasn't clear to me here — separate score? sub-score? The "93%+ collusion detection at 3+ members" claim is suspiciously precise with no cited test.
- Clicked: "Walk through a full day in Web4".

### A Day in Web4 (26:00)
- The five app mockups (Mail, Talent, Reviews, Social, Market) finally made it concrete. The "first 5 minutes" setup sequence (download app → bind device → join community → first action) is exactly what I wanted after all the theory.
- Confusion points, and this page had the most:
  - The Market mockup lists a used camera at **350 ATP**. But I started with 100 ATP, and the system keeps insisting ATP is "an energy budget, not money you accumulate." So how does anyone ever afford a 350 ATP camera? Either ATP is savable/earnable like money (contradicting the framing) or the marketplace can't work. This bugged me for the rest of the session.
  - Escrow shown as "2 ATP (reduced — high seller trust)" — reduced from what? No base cost stated.
  - The setup flow assumes my phone has a security chip; the page names three flagship phones and moves on.
- Clicked: the LCT explainer, because "device ceiling 0.85" needed explaining.

### LCT Explainer (34:00)
- Best technical page I read. It answered the exact questions I brought: why hardware (keys generated inside silicon, can't be extracted), why ceilings (a lone device has no witness), why not 1.00 ("no hardware is unbreakable"), and what happens when I lose devices (quorum recovery in hours, or social recovery in 3–7 days).
- The ceiling table (TPM 0.90 / Secure Enclave 0.85 / FIDO2 0.75 / software-only 0.50) is clear and consistent with the 0.85 iPhone figure from A Day in Web4.
- Confusion points:
  - The page calls 0.5 the "survival line — fall below and stay below = identity death." But First Contact taught me that everyone *starts* at 0.50 and that it's "neutral." So I begin my Web4 life standing exactly on the death line? That framing collision stopped me cold for a minute.
  - Worse: software-only identities have a trust *ceiling* of 0.50 — the same number as the survival line. Does that mean a person with no security chip lives permanently at death's door, unable to ever be "solidly alive"? The page presents 0.50-ceiling and 0.50-survival-line within a screen of each other and never addresses the collision. Given the site elsewhere worries about hardware equity, this seems like exactly the case that needs one honest sentence.

### ATP Economics (40:00)
- Resolved most of my money confusion: starter grant of 100 from a community pool anchored to real resources, 5% burn on transfers, quality ramp (below ~30% earns nothing, above ~70% earns full), "not a cryptocurrency — no market, no price, no mining."
- The "who decides quality?" section (confirmation speed, diversity, confirmer trust) answered my TL;DR-era question about circular validation better than I expected.
- Confusion points: it did *not* resolve the 350 ATP camera. This page says ATP is "a finite per-agent allowance, not money you accumulate" — yet the Market mockup two pages back requires accumulating 350. The two pages describe what feels like two different substances with one name.
- Small note: the site's checklist-level pairing "ATP/ADP" appears in places, but in my entire session I never learned what ADP is. ATP got a full page; ADP never got a sentence I saw.

### Karma Journey (45:00)
- The two-death distinction (energy death = recoverable suspension; trust collapse = permanent revocation) is the clarification I'd wanted since the TL;DR teased "death."
- The starting surcharge ("new players pay ~40% more because CI starts at 0.85, cost = 1/CI²") framed as "the system being honest about what it hasn't seen" — I liked this a lot. It converts a punitive-feeling mechanic into a legible one.
- Confusion points:
  - This page defines Raw Trust as "Talent 0.3 + Training 0.3 + Temperament 0.4." But the Trust Tensor page (which I read next) shows a Data Analyst role weighted 40/35/25 and says roles weight dimensions differently. Nobody tells me which weighting is *the* one, or whether Karma Journey is just one role's weighting. Two pages, two sets of numbers, no bridge sentence.
  - Within this one page, the endpoint of the survival rule wobbles: energy death is defined as "ATP depleted, raw trust **≥ 0.5**" (recoverable), but survival is stated as "raw trust must stay **above** 0.5." I start at exactly 0.500 — am I above the line or on it? One clause says I'm safe, the other implies I'm not. For a mechanic named *survival*, the boundary matters.
  - How my choices move Talent/Training/Temperament individually (all shown starting at 0.500) is never explained — the action cards show ATP deltas but not tensor deltas.

### Trust Tensor (49:00)
- Role-contextual trust (surgeon-trust ≠ cook-trust) is the most intuitively *right* idea on the site. The measurement section (Talent never decays; Training 180-day half-life; Temperament 30-day half-life) made the model feel thought-through.
- V3 vs T3 finally clicked here: V3 scores *what you produce*, T3 scores *who you are*, and V3 feeds T3.
- Confusion points: as noted above — this page's role-specific weights vs Karma Journey's fixed 0.3/0.3/0.4. If weights are role-specific, Karma Journey should say which role I'm playing.

### Running Now (52:00)
- I came skeptical: the landing page claims parts are "live today." Verdict: honest but thin. "Hestia is deployed" and "the fleet" (the lab's own AI machines) are the running things; "the hub" is carefully labeled runnable-not-running; federation is labeled "spec only, not yet built."
- The page openly says "no public network of real users for you to join yet" — I respect that.
- Confusion points: for something claimed *deployed*, there's zero operational evidence — no instance counts, no uptime, no screenshot, just GitHub links and an external fleet link. "Verify it yourself by cloning the repo" is fair for developers; it's nothing for me.

### Society Simulator (54:00)
- Skimmed rather than deeply played (end of session, and the fetches only show me the static text). The setup (12 agents, 5 strategies, presets like Hostile World) and the "what to watch for" phase guide were legible after everything I'd read.
- The "what's real / what's simplified" cards are excellent practice — it told me upfront that this demo uses a single scalar reputation, not the T3 I'd just learned, and where the real math lives.
- No new confusion beyond mild whiplash at being told the tensor I just studied is simplified away here — but the page owns that explicitly.

## Understanding Gained
By the end of this session, I understood:
- [x] What Web4 is (at a high level) — a research proposal for trust built into internet infrastructure: hardware-bound identity, energy-priced actions, earned portable reputation
- [x] What 4-Life is trying to demonstrate — an interactive lab simulating the proposal so you can feel the mechanics
- [x] What LCT means — hardware-anchored identity with trust ceilings by chip strength; witnesses and quorum recovery
- [x] What ATP means — energy budget: actions cost, confirmed quality recharges, 5% transfer burn (ADP, though — never learned; paired in name only somewhere, never explained in anything I read)
- [x] What Trust Tensors are — Talent/Training/Temperament per role, fed by V3 scores on outputs (though the two pages disagree on weights)
- [x] How agents "live" and "die" — energy death (recoverable suspension) vs trust collapse below 0.5 (permanent) — with the caveat that the exact boundary at 0.5 is stated inconsistently
- [x] Why any of this matters — spam/sybil/reset problems are real; whether this fixes them is honestly labeled as unproven

## Honest Assessment

This is the best-organized version of a hard sell I've encountered: a speculative protocol explained with unusual honesty about what doesn't exist yet. The funnel works — landing hook → 2-minute TL;DR → Alice's walkthrough → deep FAQ — and by minute 20 I understood a system I'd never heard of at breakfast. The site's habit of pre-empting my skepticism ("is this crypto? no", "is this deployed? mostly no", "could quality scores become censorship? yes, potentially") is its single strongest feature. I kept waiting for the grift moment and it never came.

The friction that remains is almost entirely *numbers that don't agree with each other across pages*. The concepts are taught well individually; it's the seams that leak. Trust weights differ between Karma Journey and Trust Tensor. The survival boundary at 0.5 is "≥" in one clause and "above" in another — on the same page — and 0.5 is simultaneously "neutral starting point," "survival line," and "the ceiling for software-only users," three framings that each make sense alone and collide when a careful reader holds them together. The Market mockup prices a camera at 3.5× my total starting energy while the economics page insists this isn't money. None of these broke my comprehension of the *ideas*; all of them dented my confidence that the *system as specified* is as tight as the prose. For a project whose core claim is "consistency is measurable and inconsistency should cost you," the site's own coherence index on its numbers is its most ironic vulnerability.

What I'd tell the authors: your prose has earned the right to be trusted; your cross-page arithmetic hasn't yet. A single pass reconciling every number that appears on two pages (weights, thresholds, ceilings, prices) would raise this from "impressively honest pitch" to "specification I'd cite."

I'd come back — mainly to actually run the Society Simulator and Karma Journey interactively rather than reading them, and to see if the 0.5 boundary question has an answer.
