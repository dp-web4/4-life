## Honest Assessment

This is one of the more thoughtful explainer sites I've wandered into. The information architecture is deliberate: a 2-minute TL;DR funnels me into a concrete story (Alice), and a learning path (Identity → Energy → Trust → Coherence → Aliveness) gave me a sense of progress. Two design choices stood out as genuinely good — the always-visible ATP/Trust numbers, and the metabolic ATP/ADP metaphor. The "Why Web4?" My recurring frustration was acronym pacing. The site clearly *knows* its terms (the glossary is excellent and defines everything), but individual pages keep deploying acronyms — V3 especially — before defining or linking them. I had to leave the flow and go term-hunting. For a naive visitor, the gap isn't that definitions are missing; it's that they're not delivered at the moment of confusion. Hover-to-define or consistent glossary-linking on first use per page would fix most of my friction in one move.

The other honest gap is at the "so what" level. I understood *what* Web4 is and *how* its mechanics work, and the problems it targets are real. But the site never convinced me it could actually happen — the adoption story is hand-wavy, the hardware assumption quietly excludes a lot of the world, and a couple of economic "gotcha" questions (unlimited ATP minting) were asserted away rather than answered. To the site's credit, it labels itself "research, not a product," so this is a forgivable gap — but if the goal is to make a visitor *care*, the leap from "this would be better" to "this is plausible" is where I disengaged slightly.

Net: I came in confused, left genuinely understanding the model, and would come back to actually play the simulators. That's a real win for an explainer about something this abstract. The work left is mostly polish (acronym delivery) and one substantive honesty upgrade (viability/adoption).
