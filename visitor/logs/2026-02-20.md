## Honest Assessment

The site has improved dramatically in its onboarding flow. The **Why Web4 page is genuinely excellent** — it's the strongest page on the site. The problem framing is compelling, the failed-solutions analysis is convincing, and the honest self-critique in the FAQ builds real trust.

The **biggest issue** is the T3 dimension inconsistency. Finding conflicting information across pages (3 dimensions vs 5 dimensions) actively undermines the credibility of the entire explanation. This needs to be fixed urgently — a visitor who notices this will wonder what else is inconsistent.

The **second biggest issue** is information density. Pages like LCT, MRH, and the glossary are thorough but overwhelming. The site needs more progressive disclosure — show the simple version first, let curious visitors drill deeper. Right now, every page tries to be both an introduction and a technical reference, and that serves neither audience well.

The **onboarding path** (landing → Why Web4 → First Contact → Playground) is well-designed and clearly signposted. The interactive tools (Playground, Society Simulator, Karma Journey) are strong learning aids. The biological metaphors (ATP, metabolism, death, rebirth) do real work making abstract concepts intuitive.

The glossary's research section (consciousness thresholds, confabulation, modal awareness) is fascinating but feels like it belongs on a different site — or at least in a clearly separated "Research" section. Mixing foundational Web4 concepts with speculative consciousness research confuses the scope of what 4-Life is.

Overall: the **conceptual foundation is solid** and the **writing quality is high**. The main barriers to understanding are structural (inconsistency, density, scope creep) rather than conceptual. A visitor with patience will come away understanding most of Web4. A visitor in a hurry will bounce after the LCT page.
