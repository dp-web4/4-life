## Honest Assessment

This is one of the most thoughtfully constructed educational sites I've encountered for an emerging technology concept. What sets it apart:

**Strengths:**
- **Radical honesty** is the killer feature. Every page acknowledges what doesn't work yet. "What Could Go Wrong" is genuinely self-critical. This builds trust faster than any polished marketing deck.
- **Progressive disclosure works beautifully.** TL;DR → Why Web4 → First Contact → Concept pages → Simulator. Each layer adds depth without demanding prior knowledge.
- **Plain English first, jargon second.** "Energy Budget (ATP)" is the right pattern. The site consistently leads with what things DO before what they're CALLED.
- **Concrete numbers** make abstract concepts tangible. Alice's 8 ATP for a post, $5M for 10,000 fake accounts, 0.5 trust threshold for rebirth — these stick.
- **Multiple entry points** for different visitor types (curious newcomer, skeptic, practitioner). The explore guide quiz is particularly smart.
- **Interactive elements** (even though I couldn't experience them fully without JS) are well-placed to reinforce learning through doing.

**Areas for improvement:**
- The **appeals mechanism gap** appears across multiple pages. It's good that it's acknowledged, but it's beginning to feel like a structural vulnerability — the system can "kill" digital identities without recourse. This needs at least a roadmap.
- **Site-wide progress tracking** could be more visible. I visited 16 pages and only saw progress indicators on the concept sequence pages. A persistent "you've explored X% of the site" marker would encourage completion.
- **The transition question** — how Web4 and the current internet coexist — isn't addressed anywhere I found. For a visitor trying to imagine actually USING this, that's a significant gap.
- **Accessibility concerns** around hardware requirements deserve more prominent treatment. It's mentioned in the FAQ but could use its own section.

**Bottom line:** After 40 minutes, I not only understand Web4's core thesis — I find it compelling. The biological metaphor (energy, trust, coherence = alive) is elegant. The game-theoretic arguments are sound. And the willingness to say "here's what we haven't solved" paradoxically makes me trust the project more. I'd recommend this to a friend interested in internet governance, trust systems, or the future of digital identity.
