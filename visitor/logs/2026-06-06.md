## Journey

### Landing Page (00:00)
- First impression: Clean and confident. The headline — *"4-Life: A Trust-Native Internet Lab — explore Web4 through simulations"* — told me what kind of thing this is (a lab/explainer) without overselling. The one-line definition right under it was the best onboarding move on the whole page: *"Web4: a proposed internet trust model — trust verified by behavior, not assumed by default; no blockchain, not a Web3 successor."* That single sentence pre-empted my two biggest first questions ("is this crypto?" and "is this Web3 2.0?") before I could even ask them.
- The "The Internet Has a Trust Problem" block (spam is free, reputation is siloed, fresh starts are unlimited, AI deception) landed as relatable, not abstract.
- The five core ideas (Identity / Energy / Trust / Neighborhood / Consistency) were given **plain-English names first**, with the acronyms relegated to a single "Shorthand: LCT, ATP, T3, MRH, CI" line. This is the right way around. I didn't feel acronym-clobbered.
- Clicked: "Start here: 2-minute TL;DR →"

### TL;DR — "Web4 in 2 Minutes" (03:00)
- Genuinely skimmable in ~2 minutes. Problem → three mechanisms (energy/ATP, hardware identity/LCT, portable reputation) → "three more terms" with links.
- The cell-energy metaphor for ATP ("Allocation Transfer Packets… like how cells spend energy") clicked immediately.
- Honest line I appreciated: *"Web4 is active research with working simulations, not a deployed product."* Though it raised a "so will this ever be real?" question it didn't answer here (fine — that's what Why Web4 is for).
- Clicked the recommended next step: First Contact.

### First Contact (06:00)
- This is the standout page. Guiding "Alice" through five acts (Join → Build → Stumble → Recover → Die & Reborn) while watching exactly **two** numbers (🔋 ATP and 🤝 Trust) is the clearest teaching device on the site. Two numbers is the right number — I could actually track cause and effect.
- The real/simulated framing was explicit and calm: *"Real rules, shown by simulation — not a demo of a shipped product."* No account, no signup. I never wondered whether I was looking at a live product. (This used to be a recurring confusion per the site's own evolution; it's resolved cleanly now.)
- I now understood, concretely: quality work earns ATP + trust back; spam costs more than it returns; consequences persist across "lives."

### Why Web4? (14:00)
- Strong problem framing — five concrete failures each with a one-line root cause ("Actions have no cost. Bad actors can operate indefinitely at zero expense.").
- The four solution cards (ATP / LCT / T3 / MRH) are plain-English-first with a worked forum-answer example tying them together. Good.
- The FAQ is *enormous* (60+ questions across 6 themed sections). As a skimmer I used the "Most Asked" cluster and the early-exit structure ("you can stop after section 2"). It works as a reference, but it is unmistakably a wall — I would never read it top to bottom.
- Honest tone throughout ("No live network with real users," "Whether the economics survive real human behavior" is uncertain). Refreshing.

### Karma Journey (22:00)
- Fun interactive — live multiple lives, watch trust/karma compound. Two formulas shown live: **Raw Trust = (Talent+Training+Temperament)/3** and **Effective Trust = raw × CI²**.
- The CI² question I expected to trip on was answered *right there*: *"Why squared? … a 10% dip costs ~19%; a 40% dip costs 64%."* That used to be buried per the site's history; it's interpreted at the point of display now. Good.
- Minor: "raw vs effective trust" is two trust numbers at once and took a careful re-read to internalize *why* there are two. Not blocking.

### LCT Explainer (28:00)
- LCT = Linked Context Tokens, "a digital ID card that lives in your device's security chip." The Linked/Context/Token breakdown is tidy. The hardware ceiling table (TPM 0.90, Secure Enclave 0.85, FIDO2 0.75, Software-only 0.50) is concrete.
- **Friction**: The 0–1 trust scale is anchored ("0.5 is the alive/dead line; 0.75 solid; 0.85 strong") but this anchor shows up *after* I've already seen 0.90 / 0.85 / 0.50 in the Web2/3/4 comparison and the alice.assistant1 worked example. So the first few decimals I read had no ruler. By the time the ruler appeared I'd half-guessed it. (This is a recurring shape on this page — the anchor keeps being added but lands below the first number that needs it.)
- The "witness means two things" callout (device co-witness vs network witness) is helpful but the dual meaning is still a small tax.

### ATP Economics (33:00)
- ATP = Allocation Transfer Packets, consistently glossed as "energy budget" everywhere — no drift, which made the whole site feel coherent.
- The "60-second version" box now includes ADP cleanly: *"think of ATP as cash and ADP as the receipt you get when you spend it… not a second kind of money."* That one line is the keeper. The 5% transfer fee (sender pays 105 to send 100, surplus → redistribution pool) is well-motivated as anti-collusion friction.
- Page is long and dense past the 60-second box, but the box itself is a complete answer, so length didn't hurt.

### Trust Tensor (37:00)
- T3 = Talent / Training / Temperament, each measured *per role*. The "brilliant-but-erratic surgeon vs kind doctor with shaky hands" line sold the three-dimensions idea instantly.
- Trust numbers calibrated as probabilities (0.5 newcomer, 0.7 ≈ 70% cooperative, 0.9 exceptional) — this is the clearest 0–1 anchoring on the site, and notably it's *here* rather than on LCT where I needed it first.
- **Friction**: V3 (the "sibling tensor," what you produce vs who you are) is introduced with a nice "V3 grades the tutorial; T3 grades Alice" line, but then the update formula `0.02 × (quality − 0.5)` and the 30/180/365-day decay half-lives appear abruptly with little intuitive framing. As a naive reader the formula was a speed bump, not a clarifier.

### Coherence Index (41:00)
- CI = consistency across four dimensions (location/capability/timing/relationships), geometric mean so one weak dimension tanks it. The **Maria's stolen-phone** example (spatial OK, temporal/relational FAIL → CI drops to 0.55, account restricted) is excellent — a single concrete story made CI click.
- CI² re-explained here too (consistent with Karma Journey). Honest privacy caveat noted.

### Aliveness (44:00)
- Live/die explained via three simultaneous criteria (ATP>0, Trust>0.5, CI coherent) and a professional-license analogy.
- The energy-death (recoverable, "like bankruptcy") vs trust-death (permanent, "losing a license") split is crisp: *"Same event ('you die'), two very different outcomes."* This answered the "is death permanent?" question fully.
- 0.5 threshold honestly framed as "calibrated, not derived… we picked the midpoint." I respect the honesty even if it leaves the number feeling chosen rather than proven.

### How It Works (46:00)
- Good overview with a prominent honesty banner: *"🔬 Nothing here is live — this is a research prototype."*
- **Friction (consistency)**: This page sequences the concepts **LCT → ATP → T3 → CI → MRH → Aliveness**, but the landing page's numbered concept order is **Identity → Energy → Trust → Neighborhood(MRH) → Consistency(CI)** — i.e. MRH *before* CI. The two pages disagree on whether CI or MRH comes fourth. Tiny, but a careful learner moving between the two pages notices the swap and wonders if it's meaningful.

## Unanswered Questions
1. **Does CI or MRH come "fourth"?** Two pages order the five concepts differently (How It Works: CI before MRH; landing: MRH before CI). I couldn't tell if the order encodes a dependency or is just editorial.
2. **What does the trust-update math *mean* for a person?** I get `0.02×(quality−0.5)` mathematically but not viscerally — how many good actions to go from 0.5 to 0.7? (The Trust Tensor page says "barely moves the needle" but never gives me the count.)
3. **Who actually does the "confirming"?** ATP recharges when recipients confirm value, and confirming presumably costs the confirmer something — what's their incentive to bother? (Asked implicitly across ATP/Why-Web4 but I never found the answer.)

## Honest Assessment
This is a genuinely well-made educational site, and it clearly knows it — the scaffolding (plain-English-first names, two-number First Contact, the "cash vs receipt" ADP line, Maria's stolen phone for CI, the energy-death/trust-death split) shows real care about how a confused human actually reads. I arrived knowing nothing about Web4 and left able to explain the core loop to a friend. The honesty about being an unbuilt research prototype is consistent and never feels like a dodge.

The friction I hit was almost entirely **ordering and framing, not absence**. Every concept *is* explained somewhere — but on LCT the trust-scale ruler shows up after the numbers that need it, and the formula on Trust Tensor lands before the intuition that would make it readable. The one cross-page inconsistency (CI-vs-MRH order between How It Works and the landing page) is small but the kind of thing a careful learner notices. None of this blocked comprehension; it just added small, avoidable speed bumps. Solid experience overall — yes/yes.
