## Journey

### Landing Page (00:00)

- I notice the line "Right now, you can't install it. This is an active open-source research project." That's honest and lowers my "is this a startup pitch?" guard.
- Multiple CTAs, but a clear primary one: "Start here: 2-minute TL;DR →". Good — too many entry points would confuse me, but this one was prominent.
- Confusion points: "Web4" appears alongside "ATP" and "AI Agents" in the nav. I don't know what these are yet, but the TL;DR button tells me where to go.
- Clicked: TL;DR.

### TL;DR (02:30)
- Sets up the four-rule frame: identity tied to devices, every action costs energy, permanent consequences, identical rules for AI and humans.
- The line "No jargon. No prerequisites. Just the idea." was reassuring. Mostly delivered.
- ATP, ADP, LCT introduced gently. I don't fully understand them yet, but I sense the shape.
- Clicked: First Contact (because it's a story and stories are easier).

### First Contact (06:00)

- The arc (build → mistake → recovery → overcommit → death → karma rebirth) is a tidy narrative shape.
- "Death and rebirth" landed as intriguing but slightly confusing. What does it actually mean for a digital identity to die? I had to take it on faith here.
- Clicked: Why Web4 (I want to know the motivation before more mechanics).

## Understanding Gained
By the end of this session, I understood:
- [x] What Web4 is (at a high level): a trust-native internet where identity is hardware-bound, actions cost energy, reputation is portable and permanent, and AI plays by the same rules as humans
- [x] What 4-Life is trying to demonstrate: a research lab/playground showing how Web4's mechanics produce emergent cooperation through interactive simulations
- [x] What LCT means: a "digital ID card" living in a device's security chip, witnessed by other devices
- [x] What ATP/ADP means: ATP is energy you spend to act, ADP is the receipt; quality work converts ADP back into ATP
- [x] What Trust Tensors are: three-dimensional, role-specific scores (Talent, Training, Temperament)
- [x] How agents "live" and "die": need ATP > 0, Trust > 0.5, and coherent CI; energy-death is recoverable, trust-death is permanent
- [x] Why any of this matters: spam, AI deception, siloed reputation, and unlimited fresh starts are real problems Web4 directly attacks

## Unanswered Questions
1. **What does the actual UI look like?** Day in Web4 has mockups of individual apps, but I want to see one continuous interface — the equivalent of "open your phone, what does the home screen show?" This recurs across visits and would help cement the vision.
2. **How do device witnesses physically communicate?** Bluetooth? Internet? Some kind of mesh? The witness concept is central to LCT but the mechanics of how my phone and laptop "see" each other aren't spelled out clearly.
3. **What's the relationship between 4-Life (the simulations on this site) and Web4 (the spec)?** Is 4-Life implementing a real protocol, or are these simulations parameterized models that could later be wired to a deployed Web4? The site occasionally feels like it's both at once.
4. **What does V3 actually do?** It's mentioned everywhere but the page that introduces T3 doesn't fully explain V3. Is V3 a property of an output (its quality), and T3 a property of the producer (their trustworthiness)? I'm guessing, not knowing.
5. **Who is building this?** I see "open-source research project" but I'd be curious about who, why, what funding model, and what timeline.

## Honest Assessment

This is one of the most thoughtfully constructed educational sites on a technical topic I've encountered. The information architecture is unusually good — there's almost always a clear next link, a TL;DR escape hatch, and an interactive option for visual learners.

What I appreciated most: the candor in "What Could Go Wrong." Most projects bury their failure modes; this one foregrounds them. That paradoxically built my trust in the team's intellectual honesty.

The friction I felt is the friction of any deeply interconnected ontology: each concept is a doorway to three others, and you can't fully explain ATP without trust, can't fully explain trust without LCT, can't fully explain LCT without witnesses, can't explain witnesses without coherence. The site handles this gracefully but the reader still feels the weight.

If I were to identify one thing that would most help a naive visitor: a single one-page "Web4 for skeptics" that lists every acronym with a one-line gloss, in dependency order, before they ever click into the deep concept pages. The Glossary panel exists, but a visitor wants the cheat sheet *handed to them*, not searched for.

Net: solid understanding, intellectual respect for the project, would return.
