## Journey

### Landing Page (00:00–03:00)
- **First impression**: "4-Life: A Trust-Native Internet Lab — explore Web4 through simulations" with subtitle "(trust verified by behavior, not assumed by default)". My very first reaction: *Web4?* I know Web3. Is this a typo? A different thing? A bigger thing? The subtitle helps slightly but is still vague.
- The hero "The Internet Has a Trust Problem" works. It's relatable.
- **Clicked**: "Start here: 2-minute TL;DR →" because that's the recommended entry and I wanted the fastest possible orientation.
- **Confusion points**:
  - "Web4" is undefined at the headline. I don't yet know whether to treat this as a marketing name, a protocol, or a research framework.
  - The five concept callouts in the nav (Linked Context Token, Trust Tensor, ATP Economics, MRH, etc.) feel like a wall of acronyms before I've earned them.
  - The phrase "Trust-Native Internet Lab" sounds like a research project — but Web4 is also being pitched as something I should care about. I'm uncertain who the site is *for*.

### TL;DR (03:00–06:30)
- **What worked**: The four bullet structure (energy actions, hardware identity, portable reputation, universal rules) is digestible. "Spam dies because it's too expensive" is a great line.
- **What didn't**: ATP is introduced as "Allocation Transfer Packets" without explanation of *why* that name. I assumed it was forced biology-flavored branding. (Later: yes, on purpose — but I had to dig three pages to find that.)
- **The line that hooked me**: "Humans and AI play by the same rules." That's actually novel and worth more emphasis up front.
- **The line that scared me**: "Active research with working simulations, not a deployed product." Honest, but I immediately wondered: *so what am I supposed to do with this? Subscribe? Wait? Build?*

2. **What do I do today?** I've read your site for 35 minutes. There's no live network. What's the call to action — read the spec? Watch for updates? Try to build something? Donate?
3. **How does a single person without three devices participate?** The recovery story is vague and 3B people have no smartphone at all.

## Honest Assessment

This is the most honest research-stage project site I've seen. The pattern of "Honest Caveat:" / "Honest Answer:" / "What Could Go Wrong" is rare and earns trust quickly. After 35 minutes I do not think this is a scam, a crypto pump, or vapor — which is a low bar but one most projects in this space fail.

That said, the site has three core problems for a naive first-time visitor:

**1. The vocabulary moat.** ATP, ADP, LCT, T3, V3, MRH, CI, Synthon, Coherence Index, Karma, Aliveness — the site is fluent in its own language and forgets that visitors aren't. Worse, several of these terms are jargon-as-flair: "Trust Tensor" is a vector; "Markov Relevancy Horizon" is a trust neighborhood (and the glossary admits it!); "Synthon" is a clique. The biology-flavored ones (ATP, ADP, aliveness, karma) are evocative but cost translation. The site should pick its battles: keep the biological metaphor for the energy system (it earns its keep) and drop the math-flavored ones (they don't).

**2. The "so what" gap.** Web4 is research. There's no deployed network. Fine. But after I understand the idea, what am I supposed to do? The TL;DR says "active research with working simulations" — and then the page ends. I don't have a next action that connects to the real world.

**3. Interactive content is gated.** The simulations (First Contact, Karma Journey, Society Simulator) are the site's strongest assets. But they're all behind "Start" buttons with no preview. Skimmers will bounce. A 5-second animated GIF before the Start button would dramatically raise engagement.

**What I'd keep**: Why Web4 (the Web3 comparison is sharp), What Could Go Wrong (foreground this!), ATP Economics' Sam/Hannah example, Trust Tensor's surgeon example, the "Honest Caveat:" pattern throughout.

**What I'd cut or rename**: "Trust Tensor" → "Trust Profile"; "Markov Relevancy Horizon" → "Trust Neighborhood"; "Synthon" → just describe it; the raw LCT identifier syntax for non-technical users.

**The single change with highest return**: Pre-define "Web4" inline on the landing page. The hero shouldn't assume I know what Web4 is — even if I know Web3, "Web4" is your coinage and you need to own it explicitly in the first sentence the visitor sees.

Final read: this is a site by serious researchers who haven't yet decided whether they're writing for other researchers or for a wider audience. Pick one. If it's researchers, lean into the formalism. If it's a wider audience, prune the vocabulary by 40%. Right now the site is in the uncanny valley between the two.
