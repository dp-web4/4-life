## Unanswered Questions
1. How would Web4 handle a mass exodus from a community? If 80% of members leave, does the remaining trust graph collapse? Does the 20% who stays retain their trust scores, or does context loss degrade them?
2. What's the actual user experience of "dying"? The site explains the mechanics but not the emotional/practical reality. Do I lose access to my messages? My history? Can I still read but not write?
3. How does the 5% transfer fee interact with collaborative work? If a team of 5 people produces something together, and ATP rewards are distributed, does each transfer within the team eat 5%?
4. The site mentions "law oracles" in SAL governance multiple times but never fully explains what they are. Are they humans? AI? Elected? Appointed?
5. If T3 Temperament has a 30-day half-life, does that mean going on a 2-month vacation tanks your trust score? What about maternity leave, illness, or imprisonment?

## Honest Assessment

This is one of the most intellectually honest project sites I've encountered. The consistent pattern of "here's our claim → here's the evidence → here's what we don't know yet" builds genuine trust in the project, which is ironic and appropriate for a project about trust.

The site does an excellent job of the "problem before solution" approach. By the time Web4 mechanisms are introduced, I already understand *why* they exist. The landing page hooks with relatable frustrations, the TL;DR gives the shape, and the deeper pages fill in detail progressively.

**What works exceptionally well:**
- The "honest caveat" pattern at the end of FAQ answers
- The "What Could Go Wrong" page — publishing your own failure analysis is a power move
- "A Day in Web4" conceptual wireframes — the moment Web4 stopped being abstract
- The four-life agent journey on the ATP page — karma made tangible
- Role-specific trust examples (Alice as analyst vs mechanic)

**What could improve:**
- Acronym management. By the time I hit the aliveness page, I was juggling 8+ abbreviations. A persistent glossary widget would help enormously.
- The T3/V3 relationship needs a clearer bridge explanation. They're clearly related but the connection isn't spelled out well.
- The "Value Confirmation" mechanism (how ATP actually recharges) is buried too deep in the journey. It answers the most natural first question — "who decides what's quality?" — but doesn't appear until the ATP economics page, which many visitors may not reach.
- Some pages promise interactivity that isn't fully delivered (First Contact, Day in Web4 scenario choices).

**Overall:** I went from "what is this?" to "oh, this is actually well thought out" over the course of 35 minutes. The site respects my time, respects my skepticism, and doesn't try to sell me anything. That's rare. Would I return? Yes — probably to try the society simulator and the karma journey. Would I recommend it? Maybe — it depends on who. A technically curious friend would love it. My non-technical parents would bounce off the acronyms by page 3.
