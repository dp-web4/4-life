## Unanswered Questions

1. How does MRH actually work in practice? I get "30% decay per hop" but what does a "hop" mean in a social network? Is it friend-of-friend distance?
2. What exactly is the rebirth/karma mechanic? Alice died but came back stronger — how is starting ATP/trust calculated from karma?
3. How does V3 (Value Tensor) differ from T3 (Trust Tensor)? Both seem to measure reputation but the glossary lists them separately.
4. What does "Coherence Index" actually measure? "Behavioral consistency across time, space, capability, and relationships" is abstract.
5. Who is building this? The site mentions "active research" and a whitepaper but I don't know the team, institution, or funding behind it.
6. What's the actual adoption pathway? The 5-tier model (Wrapper → Native) is mentioned but how would I, as a regular user, first encounter Web4 in practice?
7. How does this differ from existing reputation systems like eBay seller ratings or Stack Overflow karma? Those are also earned, persistent, and visible.

## Honest Assessment

This is one of the most thoughtful and well-structured research sites I've encountered. The onboarding path from landing page → TL;DR → personalized frustrations → deeper explainers is well-designed. I never felt lost about *where to go next*.

**What worked exceptionally well:**
- Problem-first framing. Every page started with a problem I recognized before introducing the solution.
- Radical honesty. The "What Could Go Wrong" page and the consistent "this is research, not a product" disclaimers built genuine trust (ironic for a trust project).
- Concrete examples. The $5M spam cost, Alice's ATP journey, the society simulator strategies — these grounded abstract concepts.
- Progressive disclosure. I could go as deep as I wanted without being forced through jargon.

**What still needs work:**
- Acronym overload. By page 4, I was juggling ATP, LCT, T3, MRH, CI, V3, SAL, R7, and more. The glossary helps but shouldn't be required reading.
- MRH remains the least-explained core concept. I understood ATP (energy), LCT (identity), and T3 (trust) clearly, but "context boundaries with 30% decay" never became intuitive.
- The gap between "simulation" and "reality" is acknowledged but not well-bridged. How does a 12-agent demo prove anything about billions of users?
- No human faces. Who is behind this? Research credibility matters, especially for a trust project.

**Bottom line:** I came in skeptical, and I'm leaving genuinely interested. The intellectual honesty is the strongest selling point — this feels like a real research project, not a crypto pitch. I'd bookmark it and come back to try the simulator.
