## Honest Assessment

The site is *good* — genuinely better than most explainer sites for a hard concept. The "Why Web4" page made me care, the LCT driver's-license analogy and the ATP biology analogy are excellent, and the honesty ("not a product, not a blockchain, research prototype") builds trust.

But my dominant experience was **acronym fatigue plus circular navigation**. Six abbreviations follow me onto every page, including the ones that promise plain language, so my working memory never gets to rest and actually *think*. And whenever I'm ready to go deeper on any page, it tells me to go read a *different* page first — First Contact points to Karma, Karma points to Karma-Consequences, LCT points back to the TL;DR, the Society Simulator points back to First Contact. It's a ring, and I kept feeling like the "real" explanation was always one click away, never here.

The single biggest fix: **let each page teach its own concept in plain nouns, inline, without shipping me elsewhere.** Say "energy," "trust score," "identity token" in the body; keep the acronyms in the glossary. If you did just that, this would go from "impressive but exhausting" to "actually approachable."

Second biggest fix: **show, don't describe.** The whole promise is watching trust dynamics unfold, but the pages that explain the simulation don't *run* it — they narrate it. A tiny auto-running demo embedded on How It Works or First Contact would do more than three paragraphs.

I'd come back, because the topic is interesting and the honesty earned my patience. Whether I'd recommend it depends on the friend — to a technical friend, yes; to a normal person, I'd warn them to brace for the acronyms.
