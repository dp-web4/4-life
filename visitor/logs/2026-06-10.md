## Understanding Gained

## Honest Assessment
This was one of the smoother first-time runs I can imagine having. The site clearly steers a newcomer (Landing → TL;DR → First Contact), every acronym got a plain-English gloss the first time I met it, and — crucially — I never once felt unsure whether this was a real product I was supposed to sign up for. That "is this real or imagined?" tension that I'd expect to trip up a naive visitor has been thoroughly defused with honest framing ("real rules, shown by simulation," "none of it ships today," "calibrated, not derived").

My understanding reached **solid**: I could explain Web4's core pitch, the four mechanisms, and the two kinds of death to a friend. The interactive trio (First Contact, Karma Journey, Society Simulator) is what made it *stick* rather than just *make sense*.

The single real snag was small and specific: the Aliveness page uses "MRH" bare, while every sibling page glosses it. It's the kind of inconsistency that's invisible if you've read the other pages in order but jarring if you arrive at Aliveness first (e.g., via First Contact's lifecycle link). One inline gloss closes it. Everything else was low-grade polish, not comprehension-blocking. The site continues to feel like one of the most carefully built educational explainers I've come across.
